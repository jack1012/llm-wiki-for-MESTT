# 📘 Math-Ed STT Wiki: An LLM-Driven Academic Knowledge Base

> **Official Declaration**: This project is a specialized academic knowledge system dedicated to **Mathematics Education Research**, specifically focusing on the **Secondary-to-Tertiary Transition (STT)**. It is an **LLM-driven Wiki**, functioning as an active cognitive compiler for scholarly discourse.

---

## 🎯 Research Scope & Domain
This system curates, synthesizes, and interlinks high-level academic research concerning the transition from secondary school mathematics to university-level mathematics. Key focus areas include:
- **Cognitive Transitions**: Process-Object layers, APOS theory, and concept image/definition evolution.
- **Sociocultural Transitions**: Rite of passage, changes in didactic contracts, and identity shifts.
- **Epistemological Shifts**: From intuitive/visual reasoning to formal/axiomatic proof-oriented mathematics.
- **Pedagogical Strategies**: Bridging courses, support systems, and curriculum alignment.

## 🧠 System Philosophy: The Compiler Model
Unlike traditional RAG systems, this Wiki is managed by an **LLM Compiler**.
- **Raw Input**: English academic papers and research data.
- **Synthesis Engine**: Active cross-referencing, concept merging, and contradiction management.
- **Wiki Output**: A highly structured, atomic, and interconnected knowledge graph designed for human interrogation and machine reasoning.

## 🏗️ Architecture
- `/raw/`: The **Immutable Source Layer**. Original documents sorted by type:
    - `01-articles/`: Scholarly papers and reports.
    - `02-books/`: Book chapters and textbooks.
    - `03-reader-notes/`: Human-made notes (reader summaries).
    - `04-transcripts/`: Audio/Video transcriptions.
    - `09-archive/`: Processed files.
- `/wiki/`: The **Compiled Knowledge Layer**. Atomic cards, concept nodes, and entity profiles synthesized from the raw sources.
- `/skills-docs/`: The **System Engineering Layer**. Operational guides and development logs.

## 📜 Academic Standards
- **Discourse Orientation**: All synthesized content must adhere to academic rigor and scholarly tone.
- **Language Policy**: **English Ingestion** for technical precision; **Traditional Chinese Querying** for user-centric interaction.
- **Traceability**: Every claim must be bidirectionally linked to its original source in `/raw/`.
