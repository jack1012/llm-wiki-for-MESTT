# International Congress of Mathematicians (ICM)

## Overview
The **International Congress of Mathematicians** (ICM) is the largest and most significant conference for mathematics globally, held every four years by the **International Mathematical Union** (IMU).

## Role in STT Research
The 1998 ICM held in Berlin marked a turning point for the recognition of **[[Secondary-to-Tertiary Transition]] (STT)** as a global research priority.

At this congress, **[[Miguel de Guzmán]]** and his colleagues presented leur seminal work, "**Difficulties in the Passage from Secondary to Tertiary Education**," which provided the first international, systematic framework for understanding transition challenges. This publication signaled to the broader mathematical community that student failure in first-year university mathematics was not merely a local issue or a sign of individual student weakness, but a structural "didactical transposition" problem common across different educational systems.

## Key Events
- **ICM 1998 (Berlin)**: Publication of the De Guzmán et al. three-layer model.
- **ICM 2022**: Continued discussions on mathematics education and the democratization of higher education.
