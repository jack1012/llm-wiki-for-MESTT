# Émile Durkheim

## Overview
**David Émile Durkheim** (1858–1917) was a French sociologist who is considered one of the principal architects of modern social science.

While not a mathematics education researcher, his sociological theories—particularly his study on suicide and the concept of **[[Anomie]]**—provided the foundational analytical lens for early researchers of student dropout, most notably **[[Vincent Tinto]]**.

## Influence on STT Research
In his 1973 synthesis, Tinto used Durkheim’s theory of suicide as an analogy for college dropout:
- **Anomic Dropout**: Occurs when the institutional environment lacks clear norms or when a student feels disconnected from the community (lack of integration).
- **Social Integration**: Tinto adapted Durkheim’s idea that social cohesion prevents self-destruction/withdrawal, applying it to the academic community.

## Key Concepts
- **[[Integration]]**: The degree to which individuals feel a sense of belonging to a collective.
- **[[Anomie]]**: A state of normlessness or social instability caused by the breakdown of standards and values; in STT, this occurs when students lose the "rules of the game" from high school and haven't yet mastered those of the university.
