# Kevin Crawford

## Overview
**Kevin Crawford** is a researcher in mathematics education known for his work on student conceptions and learning approaches. He is best known for a seminal 1994 study that applied **[[Phenomenography]]** to understand how entering university students conceive of mathematics.

## Key Contributions
- **Taxonomy of Mathematics Conceptions**: Identified five qualitatively distinct levels of how students view mathematics, ranging from "rules and formulas" (fragmented/naive) to "a system for understanding the world" (cohesive/complex).
- **Conception-Learning Link**: Demonstrated that a student's underlying conception of mathematics directly dictates their learning approach (surface vs. deep).
- **[[Surface Learning]] vs. [[Deep Learning]]**: Found that students with procedural conceptions (rules-based) almost exclusively use surface learning strategies, which are insufficient for tertiary mathematics.

## Research Context
His work often utilizes the Australian higher education context, focusing on the gap between secondary school training (centered on computation) and university expectations (centered on abstraction and proof).
