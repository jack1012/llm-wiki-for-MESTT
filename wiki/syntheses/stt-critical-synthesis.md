# STT Literature Critical Synthesis & Gap Analysis (批判性綜述與缺口分析)

> [!NOTE]
> This critical synthesis addresses the core problematizations (P1-P9) of the 187-paper STT corpus, identifies methodological and theoretical gaps, and explicitly bridges the global research frontier with the localized Taiwan context (e.g., Institutional Avoidance, Summer Vacuum).

## 1. Core Synthesis Problematics (The P1-P9 Matrix)
*Based on the systemic coding of 187 foundational and contemporary STT papers.*

### P1 & P2: The Dual Rupture (Cognitive & Institutional)
The literature confirms that the STT rupture is not a singular event but a multi-dimensional discontinuity.
*   **Cognitive (P1)**: The leap from *empirical-inductive* school math to *axiomatic-deductive* advanced mathematical thinking (AMT) (Tall, 1998).
*   **Institutional (P2)**: The shift in the **Didactic Contract** and institutional praxeologies (Winslow, 2009; Gueudet, 2008), where students are suddenly expected to navigate autonomous, unguided learning.
*   **Critical Synthesis**: P1 is often the symptom, while P2 is the root cause. Universities rarely adjust their praxeologies to support the cognitive leap, leading to systemic failure.

### P3 & P4: Affective Crisis and the Prediction Paradox
*   **Affective Identity Crisis (P3)**: Currently the most researched area (~23%). "First-time failure" triggers severe identity alienation (Di Martino, 2016). Dropout is repeatedly shown as an *identity protection strategy* rather than a pure lack of cognitive ability.
*   **The Prediction Paradox (P4)**: Secondary grades cannot accurately predict university survival. Contemporary research uses dynamic affective trajectories and early semester behaviors to predict attrition (Geisler, 2023).

### The Missing 6th Dimension: Career Development (生涯發展)
Traditional literature over-relies on "Deficit" or "Collision" models, largely ignoring student agency. This synthesis introduces **Career Development** as the critical 6th dimension. 
When the "instrumental value" of abstract mathematics (e.g., Calculus, Linear Algebra) cannot be mapped to a student's envisioned career path, their resilience against P1/P2/P3 challenges collapses, leading to active dropout or avoidance strategies.

## 2. Theoretical & Methodological Paradigms (Evolution)

### Theoretical Evolution
1.  **1990s (Diagnostic Phase)**: Individual Cognition (Tall) & Sociological Integration (Tinto).
2.  **2000s (Explanatory Phase)**: Institutional Praxeologies (Artigue, Winslow).
3.  **2010s (Action Phase)**: Identity Construction (Hernandez-Martinez) & Affective Tracking.
4.  **2020s+ (Ecosystem Phase)**: **Theory Networking** (Gueudet, 2023), AI-driven Early Warning Systems, and Global South/Cross-Cultural narratives (P9).

### Methodological Trends & Gaps
*   **The Shift**: From cross-sectional surveys/interviews to **Longitudinal Experience Sampling (ESM)** and **Survival Analysis** of large-scale national databases.
*   **Sampling Bias (P8)**: Heavy over-reliance on traditional "Math Majors" at elite Western universities. High-achieving females, students with learning disabilities (LD), and indigenous populations are structurally under-represented.

## 3. Global Research Gaps & Taiwan Implications (P9)

### Global Gaps
1.  **Teacher Blind Spots (P6)**: Why do university lecturers maintain "survival of the fittest" pedagogies despite clear evidence of STT crises? The literature lacks intervention studies aimed at changing lecturer praxeologies.
2.  **Intervention Paradox (P7)**: Bridging courses often suffer from the "Matthew Effect"—they help those who need it least and fail to reach or retain the most vulnerable students.

### Deep Implications for Taiwan Context (台灣在地脈絡)
Taiwan's unique institutional and socio-cultural environment significantly rewrites the STT model:

*   **Institutional Avoidance (制度性規避)**: Under Taiwan's strict "Double-21" (雙二一) expulsion rules, students actively engage in strategic course withdrawals (Tao, 2021) to survive. Dropout here is not a passive failure but a calculated, path-correcting maneuver.
*   **The Summer Vacuum (暑假真空期)**: The lengthy gap between the intense high-school entrance exams and the start of university creates an epistemological void. Upon entering, students experience an immediate clash between their "cram-school procedural mastery" and the university's demand for theoretical rigor.
*   **Double Discontinuity for Pre-Service Teachers**: Taiwanese pre-service math teachers suffer severe STT trauma at university, which they often subconsciously carry back to the secondary classroom, perpetuating the cycle (Wasserman, 2023).

> [!TIP]
> **Doctoral Research Trajectory**: Future studies in Taiwan should pivot toward **Survival Analysis modeling** that incorporates "Career Uncertainty" and "Institutional Avoidance", moving beyond Western-centric cognitive deficit models to accurately map the East Asian STT reality.
