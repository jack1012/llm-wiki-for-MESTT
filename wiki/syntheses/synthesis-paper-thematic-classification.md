## Strategic Insights

### 1. Research Cluster Density
Largest clusters: Affective/Identity (31) and STT Empirical (25), reflecting the field current affective turn (2015-2024).

### 2. Temporal Evolution
- 1970s-1990s: Foundation building (Tinto, Crawford, De Guzman)
- 2000s: Framework integration (Gueudet, Artigue, Chevallard)
- 2010s: Affective turn + empirical expansion (Geisler, Rach, Di Martino)
- 2020s: Synthesis and diversity

### 3. Taiwan Context Gap
40 papers (21%) are Taiwan-specific, providing rich local context but largely disconnected from international STT theory.

### 4. Key Author Networks
- Geisler-Rach axis (Hannover): Beliefs, attitudes, dropout (12 papers)
- Di Martino (Pisa): Affective crisis, identity (3 papers)
- Hernandez-Martinez (Loughborough): Identity, resilience (5 papers)
- Gueudet-Artigue (France): Theoretical frameworks (6 papers)
