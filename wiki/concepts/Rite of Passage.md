# Concept: {{title}}

## 📖 Definition
A conceptual framework (originally from anthropology) applied to the secondary-tertiary transition by Clark and Lovric (2008). It frames the transition as a transformative process involving a change in status and identity.

## 🏗️ Three Stages
1. **Separation**: Disconnecting from the previous high school routine and social status.
2. **Liminal (Threshold)**: An intermediate phase characterized by ambiguity, distortion of routines, and the **[[Concept/Transition Shock]]**.
3. **Incorporation**: Being integrated into the university culture with a new mathematical identity.

## ⚠️ The Transition Crisis
The rite of passage is inherently characterized by a "crisis" where the individual's previous competence is questioned. Di Martino & Gregorio (2019) emphasize that this shock is "inevitable."
