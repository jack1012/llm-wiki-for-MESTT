# Sociological/Cultural Difficulties in Education

## Definition
This category of **[[Secondary-to-Tertiary Transition]]** challenges focuses on the external, environmental, and interpersonal factors that disrupt a student's sense of belonging and ability to function within the university system. It is one of the three layers in **[[Miguel de Guzmán]]'s** (1998) model of STT difficulties.

## Key Dimensions
1. **Institutional Scale**: The jump from small, intimate high school classes (10–30 students) to large, anonymous university lecture halls (100–500+ students). This leads to **anonymization** and a perceived lack of teacher support.
2. **Community Culture**: A shift from a "cooperative/supportive" culture in secondary school to a "competitive/individualistic" culture at the university level.
3. **Student Heterogeneity**: Entering cohorts have increasingly diverse backgrounds, prior knowledge levels, and motivations, making standardized instruction less effective.
4. **Identity and Role Change**: The student must shift from being a "driven" pupil to a **[[Self-Directed Learning|self-directed learner]]**.

## Relevance to STT
These difficulties often produce **[[Identity Shock]]** or an **[[Affective-Identity Crisis|affective crisis]]** (**[[Pietro Di Martino|Di Martino 2018]]**). Even if a student has the cognitive ability to do the math, sociological factors like isolation or the lack of a "mentor" can lead to voluntary withdrawal.
