# Concept: {{title}}

## 📖 Definition
A psychological phenomenon where high-achieving students, who have never experienced academic struggle in school, face a series of "first-time" failures or difficulties upon entering university (e.g., first failed exam, first lecture they don't understand).

## ⚠️ Impact on STT
In the mathematics transition, this syndrome triggers intense negative emotions (shame, anxiety) and often leads to a collapse of the student's mathematical identity. The inability to cope with "being average" or "struggling" is a major driver of early dropout among excellent students (Di Martino & Gregorio, 2019).
