# Mathematical Language and Notation

## Definition
**Mathematical Language and Notation** refers to the system of symbols, syntax, and formal conventions used to express mathematical ideas and proofs concisely and precisely. Mastery of this language is a fundamental prerequisite for **[[Advanced Mathematical Thinking]]**.

## The "Foreign Language" Problem in STT
During the **[[Secondary-to-Tertiary Transition]]**, many students experience mathematical language as a "foreign language" (**[[Ghislaine Gueudet|Gueudet 2008]]**).
- **Notation Overload**: Move from a few standard notations (e.g., $f(x)$) to a dense forest of symbols ($\forall$, $\exists$, $\in$, $\subset$, $\implies$).
- **Semantic Shifts**: Familiar words in secondary school (e.g., "limit," "function") take on much more rigid, formal definitions in university.
- **Syntactic vs. Semantic Proof**: Students often struggle with the "syntax" of writing a proof (where to put the symbols) before they can even engage with the "semantics" (the underlying logic).

## Role in Dropout
Failure to "decode" mathematical language leads to massive **[[Cognitive Discontinuity]]**. Students may feel that "this isn't the math I signed up for" (**[[Di Martino 2018]]**), leading to an **[[Affective-Identity Crisis]]**.
