# Practical vs. Theoretical Thinking

## Definition
Developed in **[[Ghislaine Gueudet]]'s** (2008) multidimensional framework, this concept describes the duality of thinking modes required in university mathematics.

1. **Practical Thinking Mode**: Example-based, intuitive, and centered on calculations and applications. This is the dominant mode in secondary education.
2. **Theoretical Thinking Mode**: Based on formal definitions, axioms, and logical proof. It requires a level of abstraction where a "result" is only valid if derived through the system's logic, regardless of intuition.

## The STT Duality Challenge
A successful transition requires students to **flex** between these two modes.
- **The Gap**: Entering students often try to solve theoretical problems (e.g., proving a limit) using only practical tools (calculating values), which leads to failure.
- **The Ideal State**: A student who can use **Practical Thinking** to generate a hunch or an example, then immediately switch to **Theoretical Thinking** to formalize and prove that hunch.

## Connection to other Frameworks
- Matches **[[Advanced Mathematical Thinking]]** (Tall): School math is often practical; university math is theoretical.
- Aligns with **[[Didactical Transposition]]**: The way math is "transposed" in high school emphasizes the practical, while the academic version is theoretical.
