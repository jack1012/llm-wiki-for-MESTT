# Institutional Fit

## Definition
**Institutional Fit** refers to the degree of congruence between a student's personal characteristics, needs, goals, and values, and the climate, resources, and expectations of the institution they attend. It is a central concept in **[[Student Integration Theory]]**.

## Indicators of Good Fit
- **Academic Alignment**: The university’s pedagogical style matches the student’s learning approach.
- **Social Compatibility**: The student finds a community that shares their interests and values.
- **Goal Support**: The institution provides the specific resources (e.g., math support centers) that the student needs for their degree path.

## Relevance to STT
In the **[[Secondary-to-Tertiary Transition]]**, many students face a "mismatch" rather than a "deficit."
- **[[Didactical Contract]]**: If a student is used to the secondary contract (highly supportive, procedural) but the university uses a formal contract (autonomous, abstract), the lack of **Institutional Fit** may lead to withdrawal.
- **Predictors of Success**: High school GPA is a measure of general ability, but **Institutional Fit** explains why two students with the same GPA may have different outcomes at the same college.
