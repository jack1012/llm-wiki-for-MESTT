## Definition

The distinction between **Stopout** (temporary withdrawal with intention to return) and **Dropout** (permanent departure from higher education) is a foundational conceptual clarification in student attrition research. [[Vincent Tinto]] (1973) identified the conflation of these two phenomena as one of the most damaging methodological flaws in early dropout research.

## Conceptual Differences

| Dimension | Stopout (暫時休學) | Dropout (永久退學) |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Intent** | Temporary; plans to return | Permanent; exits the system |
| **Cause** | Often strategic (financial, personal, health) | Often reflects integration failure |
| **Outcome** | Delayed completion | No completion |
| **STT implication** | "Benign pause" in transition | Transition failure |

## STT Relevance

In the STT context, the stopout-dropout distinction is critical for accurate diagnosis. A first-year mathematics student who withdraws after a semester of [[Mathematical Crisis]] may be:
- **Stopping out**: Taking time to recover emotionally and cognitively, possibly returning with better strategies — this reflects adaptive coping.
- **Dropping out**: Permanently abandoning STEM, driven by irreversible identity damage — this reflects transition failure.

University retention interventions that treat all departures identically will waste resources trying to "retain" students who would benefit from a strategic pause, while failing to identify those at genuine risk of permanent attrition.
