## Definition

Epistemological Mismatch refers to the fundamental disconnect between a student's conception of what mathematics *is* (and how it should be learned) and the epistemological demands of university-level mathematics. The concept was empirically identified by [[Kathryn Crawford]] (1994), who demonstrated that students entering university predominantly hold **fragmented** conceptions of mathematics (a collection of disconnected rules and formulas), while university mathematics requires **cohesive** conceptions (an interconnected logical system).

## The Mismatch Mechanism

Crawford's phenomenographic analysis revealed a two-level mismatch:

1. **Ontological level**: Students believe mathematics *is* a set of procedures to memorize. University mathematics treats it as a logical structure to construct and prove.
2. **Strategic level**: Students approach learning through [[Surface Learning Approach]] (rote memorization, pattern reproduction). University success requires [[Deep Learning]] (meaning-seeking, relational understanding).

The mismatch is particularly insidious because the fragmented/surface approach was *highly successful* in high school — students earned top marks by memorizing formulas and drilling practice problems. This prior success reinforces the flawed epistemology, making the university collision even more shocking.

## STT Relevance

Epistemological Mismatch is one of the most powerful explanatory concepts for first-year STEM failure. It explains the paradox of the "high school star who fails university":
- The student is not lacking ability (they excelled in high school mathematics).
- The student is not lacking effort (they may study *more* hours than in high school).
- The student is using a fundamentally inappropriate *way of knowing* — their entire epistemological framework is misaligned with the new cognitive demands.

This concept connects directly to:
- [[Miguel de Guzmán]]'s (1998) third barrier: the [[Cognitive-Language Barrier]] between intuitive-heuristic and formal-axiomatic mathematical language.
- [[Ghislaine Gueudet]]'s (2008) cognitive dimension: the shift from [[Practical vs Theoretical Thinking]].
- Di Martino's (2018) [[Trap of Effort Belief]]: when effort fails because the effort is applied through the wrong epistemological lens.
