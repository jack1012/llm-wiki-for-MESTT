## Definition & Core Idea
> Mathematical tasks that do not have a straightforward, previously rehearsed algorithmic solution, forcing students to engage in higher-order thinking and conceptual reasoning.

## Key Dimensions / Components
- **Example Generation**: Asking students to generate examples matching specific criteria.
- **Analyzing Reasoning**: Asking students to evaluate proofs or arguments.
- **Conjecturing & Visualizing**: Requiring flexible thinking over simple computation.

## ⚙️ Application in STT (Transition)
- In STT, non-routine tasks break the "surface learning" habits acquired in secondary school and signal the need for deep, relational understanding in university mathematics.
