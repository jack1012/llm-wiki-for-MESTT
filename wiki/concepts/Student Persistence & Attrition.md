# Student Persistence & Attrition

## Definition
Direct outcomes of the college experience, **Persistence** refers to a student's continued enrollment until degree completion, while **Attrition** (or Dropout) refers to the premature departure from an institution or the higher education system.

## Distinct Forms of Attrition
Tinto (1973, 1975) emphasizes the importance of distinguishing between different types of departure:
1. **Voluntary Withdrawal**: A student chooses to leave, often due to poor **[[Institutional Fit]]** or changes in personal goals.
2. **Academic Dismissal**: The institution forces the student to leave due to failure to meet academic standards.
3. **Transfer**: The student leaves one institution to persist at another (system persistence but institutional attrition).
4. **Permanent Dropout**: The student leaves the higher education system entirely.

## Predictors and Factors
- **Integration**: High levels of **[[Academic Integration in Higher Education|academic]]** and **[[Social Integration in Higher Education|social]]** integration are the strongest predictors of persistence.
- **Commitment**: Persistence is driven by **[[Goal vs. Institutional Commitment|goal commitment]]** (desire for degree) and institutional commitment (desire to stay *this* college).
- **Background Variables**: Family background, prior schooling, and individual abilities set the starting point for the persistence process.

## Relevance to STT
The **[[Secondary-to-Tertiary Transition]]** is a "high-attrition" window. STT research focuses on identifying the specific levers (e.g., **[[Bridging Course|Bridging Courses]]**, **[[Mathematics Support Centre|Mathematics Support Centers]]**) that can convert potential attrition into successful persistence during the first critical year.
