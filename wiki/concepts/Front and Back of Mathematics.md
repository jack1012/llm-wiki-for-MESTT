# Concept: {{title}}

## 📖 Core Idea
Theoretical distinction introduced by Philip Hersh (1991):
- **Front**: Mathematics in its polished, finished form (theorems and proofs as presented in textbooks).
- **Back**: The internal, messy mental process of discovering and proving (including failures and dead ends).

## ⚠️ Transition Conflict
Traditional lectures often only show the "Front," leading students to develop static beliefs. Accessing the "Back" is necessary for developing advanced mathematical thinking (Geisler & Rolka, 2021).
