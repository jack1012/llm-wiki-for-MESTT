## Definition

Disciplinary Heartbreak is [[Pietro Di Martino]]'s (2018) powerful metaphor for the emotional experience of STT transition failure: the student's relationship with mathematics — once a source of pride, identity, and pleasure — undergoes a painful "breakup." The construct captures the profound sense of loss, betrayal, and grief that accompanies the shift from mathematical confidence to mathematical crisis.

## The Relationship Metaphor

Di Martino conceptualizes the student-mathematics relationship as analogous to a romantic relationship:

1. **Honeymoon phase (高中)**: Mathematics is reliable, rewarding, and affirming. The student loves math, math "loves" them back (high grades, positive feedback, identity as "the math person").

2. **Crisis phase (大一轉銜)**: The relationship suddenly becomes hostile. Mathematics becomes unintelligible, punishing, and humiliating. The old "love language" (memorization, procedural fluency) no longer works.

3. **Breakup phase (退學/轉系)**: The student experiences a painful severance — not just from a course, but from a core part of their identity. Dropping mathematics is experienced as a personal loss, not merely an academic decision.

## STT Relevance

Disciplinary Heartbreak is significant because it highlights the **identity dimension** of STT failure that purely cognitive frameworks miss. When a student who has been "the math genius" throughout secondary school encounters university-level failure, they lose not just grades but **self**:

- "If I'm not good at math, who am I?"
- "Mathematics was the one thing I was sure about. Now I'm sure about nothing."

This identity crisis connects directly to:
- [[Trap of Effort Belief]]: The effort-failure disconnect is experienced as "betrayal" — the discipline broke its promise.
- [[Mathematical Crisis]]: Disciplinary Heartbreak is the subjective emotional experience of the broader mathematical crisis.
- [[Learned Helplessness in Math]]: Repeated failure after heartbreak produces learned helplessness, completing the withdrawal spiral.

The concept also has profound career implications: a student who experiences Disciplinary Heartbreak in mathematics may generalize the emotional trauma to the entire STEM field, leading to permanent career redirection away from quantitative disciplines.
