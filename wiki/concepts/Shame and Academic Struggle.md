# Shame and Academic Struggle

## Definition
A specific affective dimension within the **[[Secondary-to-Tertiary Transition]]**, particularly prevalent among **high-achieving secondary students**. It refers to the intense emotional distress and self-blame that occurs when a student’s identity as "mathematically talented" is shattered by university-level failure or struggle.

## The High-Achiever Trap
**[[Pietro Di Martino]]** (2018) identified that:
- **Low-level struggle experience**: High-achieving students often cruised through secondary school without ever facing true mathematical failure.
- **Identity Collision**: When they hit the "wall" of abstract algebra or analysis, they don't see it as a difficult task, but as a proof of their own "fraudulence" or loss of talent.
- **The Shame Spiral**: Unlike guilt (feeling one did something wrong), shame (feeling one *is* wrong/inadequate) leads to withdrawal. Students stop asking questions and stop attending labs to hide their shame, which guarantees actual failure.

## Relevance to STT
Shame is a major driver of "voluntary" dropout. Institutional interventions like **[[Mathematics Support Centre|Math Support Centers]]** or **peer mentoring** aim to normalize struggle and de-stigmatize failure, helping students transition their identity from "innately talented" to "strategically persistent."
