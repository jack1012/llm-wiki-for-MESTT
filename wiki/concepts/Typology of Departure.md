# Typology of Departure

## Definition
The **Typology of Departure** is a categorical framework developed by **[[Vincent Tinto]]** to distinguish between the different ways and reasons students leave higher education. Tinto argued that treatng all "dropout" as a single phenomenon is analytically flawed because the causes and remedies vary significantly between types.

## The Four Main Types
1. **Voluntary Withdrawal**: A student who is academically successful but chooses to leave because they do not feel a sense of **[[Social Integration in Higher Education|social]]** or **[[Academic Integration in Higher Education|academic]]** belonging, or because they have found a better **[[Institutional Fit]]** elsewhere.
2. **Academic Dismissal**: The student is forced to leave by the institution due to poor performance (failure to integrate academically).
3. **Transfer**: The student leaves the current institution but immediately enrolls in another. This is "institutional failure" but "system success."
4. **Permanent Dropout**: The student leaves higher education entirely and does not return to any institution.

## Relevance to STT
STT research uses this typology to understand why students leave mathematics programs.
- **Cognitive Attrition**: Often leads to **Academic Dismissal** (unable to master the abstraction jump).
- **Affective Attrition**: Often leads to **Voluntary Withdrawal** (high-achieving students who feel shame or loss of identity; see **[[Shame and Academic Struggle]]**).
