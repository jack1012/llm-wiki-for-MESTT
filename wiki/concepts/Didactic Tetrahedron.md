## Definition
An expansion of the classical **Didactic Triangle** (Teacher, Student, Mathematics) proposed by Sebastian Rezat (2006, 2014). It adds the **Artifact** (e.g., textbook, digital tool) as a fourth vertex, creating a three-dimensional space of interaction.

## Interaction Faces
1. **Student-Teacher-Mathematics**: The traditional pedagogical interaction.
2. **Student-Mathematics-Artifact**: How the student learns math through a tool.
3. **Teacher-Mathematics-Artifact**: How the teacher uses the tool to represent math.
4. **Student-Teacher-Artifact**: The social and mediational use of the tool between people.

## Significance in STT
In the Secondary-Tertiary Transition, the "Artifact" vertex often changes drastically (e.g., from a colorful, guided textbook to a dense, black-and-white university script), contributing to the transition shock.
