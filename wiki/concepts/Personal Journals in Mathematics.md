## Definition
**Personal Journals in Mathematics** refer to the practice of students maintaining a regular narrative record of their learning experiences, emotional states, and cognitive struggles within a mathematics course.

## Role in STT
As demonstrated in the research of [[Giulia Bernardi]], personal journals serve two primary roles during the [[Secondary-to-Tertiary Transition (STT)]]:
1.  **Diagnostic Tool for Educators**: Allowing instructors to see beyond grades and understand students' social and cultural adjustment difficulties.
2.  **Metacognitive Tool for Students**: Helping students reflect on their learning strategies, regulate their emotions, and navigate the [[Liminality in STT|liminal space]] of the transition.
