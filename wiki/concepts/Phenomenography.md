# Phenomenography

## Definition
**現象描述學 (Phenomenography)** 是一種質性研究方法，致力於描述人們如何「經歷、理解、感知或概念化」世間現象的各種方式（Categories of Description）。它不關注現象本身的本質，而是關注群體中對該現象理解的**變異性 (Variation)**。

## Application in STT Research
In mathematics education, phenomenography is the primary tool for identifying student **conceptions**.
- **[[Kevin Crawford]]** (1994) applied this methodology to categorise the five levels of **[[Mathematics Conceptions (Epistemology)|mathematical conceptions]]** among entering university students.
- **Outcome Space**: The result of a phenomenographic study is a structured "outcome space" that shows how different understandings are logically and hierarchically related (e.g., from procedural to structural).

## Key Characteristics
- **Non-dualist view**: Research is focused on the relationship between the subject (student) and the object (mathematics).
- **Intersubjective**: Findings describe the shared space of meanings within a specific group (e.g., first-year math majors).
