# Concept: {{title}}

## 📖 Definition
A grounded theoretical model developed by Di Martino and Zan (2010) to define student attitude towards mathematics. It moves beyond simple "positive/negative" labels by integrating three interacting dimensions.

## 🏗️ The Three Dimensions
1. **Emotional Disposition**: The emotional reaction towards mathematics (e.g., enjoyment, anxiety).
2. **Vision of Mathematics**: The student's epistemological beliefs about what mathematics is (e.g., rules vs. reasoning).
3. **Perceived Competence**: The student's self-perception of their ability to succeed in mathematics.

## 📉 Relevence to Transition
Di Martino & Gregorio (2019) use this model to show that transition crises often involve a "revolution" in the Vision of Mathematics and a collapse of Perceived Competence.
