# Concept: {{title}}

## 📖 Definition
A psychological framework (primarily developed by Bernard Weiner) that studies how individuals interpret causes for events (success or failure) and how these interpretations affect their future behavior and motivation.

## 🏗️ Three Main Dimensions
1. **Locus of Control**: Internal vs. External.
2. **Stability**: Stable vs. Unstable.
3. **Controllability**: Controllable vs. Uncontrollable.

## 📉 Relevence to Transition
Successful transition students tend to attribute failure to **unstable and controllable** factors (e.g., effort, strategy). Dropout students often attribute it to **internal and stable** factors (e.g., lack of innate talent), leading to resignation (Di Martino & Gregorio, 2019).
