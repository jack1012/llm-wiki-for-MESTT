# Concept: {{title}}

## 📖 Definition
The degree to which incoming university students possess the necessary technical skills, analytical powers, and conceptual understanding required to succeed in tertiary mathematics courses.

## 📉 Concerns in Literature
Gruenwald et al. (2003) cite a joint report noting an "unprecedented concern" regarding the lack of preparedness in new undergraduates, specifically in:
1. Essential technical facility (calculation fluency).
2. Analytical powers for multi-step problems.
3. Appreciation for mathematical precision and proof.
