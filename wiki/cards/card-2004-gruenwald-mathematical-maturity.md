# Gruenwald 2004 - Mathematical Maturity

## Definition
Gruenwald et al. define **Mathematical Maturity** as the holistic ability to:
1.  Handle **abstract mathematical objects** with confidence.
2.  Understand and construct **rigorous proofs**.
3.  Acquire a logical "feeling" for mathematical structure.
4.  Master **formal mathematical language and notation**.

## Critical Role in STT
The paper argues that transition success depends more on achieving this "maturity" than on accumulating more procedural knowledge. 

## Comparison with School Math
Secondary math focuses on calculation; tertiary math focuses on maturation—the ability to sit with an abstract concept until its logical structure becomes clear. Failure to move toward this maturity results in students attempting to "memorize proofs," which is unsustainable at the university level.
