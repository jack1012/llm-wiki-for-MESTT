# Klymchuk 2014 - Mutual Awareness Deficit

## The Concept
Klymchuk et al. (2014) identifies a **Lack of Mutual Awareness** as the root cause of many transition difficulties.

## The Two Blind Spots
1.  **Secondary Blindness**: Teachers often do not know the level of abstract rigor expected at university. Consequently, they "over-protect" students by simplifying concepts into procedurally safe algorithms to ensure high grades.
2.  **Tertiary Blindness**: Lecturers often do not know the extreme pressure of national standardized testing (like NCEA) on high schools. They blame teachers for "bad habits" without understanding the institutional constraints that force them.

## Result
Students are caught in the middle. They arrive at university with "A" grades but no idea how to perform the **[[Independent Learning]]** that lecturers assume they already know.
