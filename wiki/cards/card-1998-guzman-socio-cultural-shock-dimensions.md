# Guzman 1998 - Socio-cultural Shock Dimensions

## The Concept
Guzman (1998) identifies specific social-cultural shifts that trigger student failure.

## The Dimensions
- **Loss of Guidance**: In secondary school, the teacher acts as a "constant monitor." In university, the student becomes an **anonymous learner**.
- **Autonomy Gap**: Most first-year students have never been taught how to organize 15-20 hours of independent study per week.
- **Rigor Pride**: The university culture often prides itself on a "sink or swim" mentality (filtering), while the student culture expects "support for success."

## Result
This "shock" leads to students feeling like outsiders in the math department, leading back to the **[[Mathematical Identity]]** crisis.
