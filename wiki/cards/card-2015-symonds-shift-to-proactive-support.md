# Symonds 2015 - Shift to Proactive Support

## The Concept
Based on the failure of voluntary support usage by at-risk groups, Symonds (2015) argues for a shift towards **Proactive Institutional Support**.

## Strategic Shifts
1.  **Embedded Support**: Integrating support sessions directly into the student's timetable, making them a "normal" part of the course rather than an "extra" choice.
2.  **Early Warning Systems**: Using diagnostic tests or mid-term results to *invite* specific students to support, rather than waiting for them to recognize their own need.
3.  **Active Outreach**: Tutors visiting lecture halls or "math cafes" to meet students in their own territory, breaking the "shame" of the office door.

## Significance in STT
Proactive support reduces the **[[Cognitive Discontinuity]]** by providing a safety net that catches students *before* their **[[Mathematical Self-concept]]** collapses.
