## Card: Self-Directed Learning as STT Critical Transition

**Source**: De Guzmán, Hodgson, Robert & Villani (1998), with Zucker's insight

### The Core Transition

High school mathematics learning:
- **Teacher-directed**: Instructor controls pace, content, scope, assessment
- **Passive reception**: "Learning must come down to me from the teacher"
- **Structured support**: Homework, practice problems, teacher corrections

University mathematics learning:
- **Student-directed**: Individual must plan study, select resources, assess understanding
- **Active construction**: "I must learn independently outside the classroom"
- **Minimal scaffolding**: Large lectures, limited feedback, self-organization required

### The Critical Gap

**Zucker's Observation** (1996):
> "The fundamental problem is that most high-school graduates don't know how to learn or what it means to learn (a fortiori to understand) something... They graduate feeling learning must come down to them from teachers... That the students must also learn on their own, outside the classroom, is **the main feature that distinguishes college from high school**."

### Why Students Fail

Many students arriving at university:
- Do not know how to take lecture notes effectively
- Cannot read mathematical textbooks independently
- Lack ability to organize knowledge hierarchically
- Cannot self-diagnose learning gaps
- Have no strategies for personal problem-solving

The transition from **dependent to autonomous learning** is **never explicitly taught** at secondary level.

### Related Phenomenon

De Guzmán identifies a "culture gap" in student expectations:
- High school: mathematics success = procedural mastery (if you can solve these problems, you understand)
- University: mathematics success = conceptual depth (you must understand underlying structures)
- **Problem**: Students still use high school success metrics and get blindsided when they don't work

### Intervention Implication

Simply providing more support (tutoring, help centers, extra classes) **is insufficient** if students lack self-directed learning capacity. Must teach:
- Meta-cognitive awareness (how to diagnose your own misunderstanding)
- Study methods suited to mathematics (different from history or biology)
- Knowledge organization skills
- Resource usage (textbooks, libraries, internet)
