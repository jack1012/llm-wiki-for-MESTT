## Definition
The tension experienced by university lecturers between the need to maintain the "high rigor" of formal mathematics and the necessity of providing support (scaffolding) for students who are not yet prepared for such abstraction.

## Key Conflict
- **Institutional Pressure**: University mathematics must be rigorous, axiomatic, and deductive to be "valid."
- **Student Reality**: Students struggle with formal proofs and often rely on informal, intuitive reasoning.
- **The Dilemma**: Providing too much scaffolding may "lower the bar" and hide the true nature of mathematics, while providing too little leads to mass failure and student alienation.

## Career Perspective
> [!NOTE]
> **專業韌性的養成**：轉銜不只是學會公式，而是學會如何承受「抽象的重壓」。成功的支持系統應引導學生經歷這種兩難，而非替他們消除兩難。
