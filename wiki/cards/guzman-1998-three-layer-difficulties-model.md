## Card: Three-Layer Difficulties Model in STT

**Source**: De Guzmán, Hodgson, Robert & Villani (1998), *Difficulties in the Passage from Secondary to Tertiary Education*

### Core Model Structure

STT difficulties manifest simultaneously across **three distinct layers**:

**1. Epistemological/Cognitive Layer**
- Calculation → Logical proof (shift from procedural to deductive thinking)
- Intuition → Formalization (e.g., ε-δ limit definition becomes necessary)
- Isolated concepts → Interconnected knowledge networks
- Unifying & generalizing concepts (vector spaces, formal algebra structures) introduce new abstraction levels
- Student tendency toward simplification (reducing to algorithms) vs. required conceptual depth

**2. Sociological/Cultural Layer**
- Class size leap: 10-30 → 50-200+ students (anonymization, loss of personal contact)
- Culture shift: cooperation → competition (mathematics as screening mechanism)
- Heterogeneous student body post-democratization (diverse backgrounds, abilities)
- Mathematics as service subject identity crisis (engineering/economics students see no connection to career)
- Teacher accessibility collapse: high school caring → university research-focused distance

**3. Didactical Layer**
- Teacher preparation deficit: university educators trained as researchers, not teachers
- Perverse incentive structure: research rewarded, teaching marginalized
- Pedagogical gaps: lack of course design skills, feedback mechanisms, assessment innovation
- Teaching method monotony: lecture-based only ("teacher talks, student writes")
- Material support void: no standardized textbooks, students scramble for notes

### Key Insight

**These three layers interact**. A student facing:
- High cognitive discontinuity + 
- Large anonymous class + 
- Unprepared teacher

...is far more likely to struggle than one with support in any single dimension.

### Why It Matters

This framework shifted STT research from single-cause thinking ("students lack ability" OR "teaching is bad") to **systems thinking**. Solutions must address all three simultaneously.
