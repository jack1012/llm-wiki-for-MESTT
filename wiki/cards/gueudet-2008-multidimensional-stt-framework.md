## Multidimensional Framework of Secondary-Tertiary Transition

**Source**: Gueudet, G. (2008). Investigating the Secondary–Tertiary Transition. Educational Studies in Mathematics, 67, 237-254.

### Core Insight
STT is not a single-dimensional problem (e.g., "students lack ability"), but a **multifaceted phenomenon** requiring simultaneous analysis of three interconnected dimensions:

### Three Analytical Dimensions

**1. COGNITIVE DIMENSION** (Individual thinking)
- Focus: How students' mathematical thinking must evolve
- Transition: From practical/procedural thinking → theoretical/proof-based thinking
- Challenge: Most students enter with only practical thinking mode; university requires both
- Source: Tall's AMT (Advanced Mathematical Thinking) framework

**2. SOCIAL DIMENSION** (Community practices)
- Focus: How students learn implicit rules of mathematical community
- Transition: From individual problem-solving → participation in mathematical discourse
- Challenge: University mathematics has unspoken norms (proof standards, notation usage, argumentation patterns) that differ from secondary
- Mechanism: Sociomathematical norms (Yackel & Cobb)—cultural standards that must be absorbed

**3. INSTITUTIONAL DIMENSION** (Educational structures)
- Focus: How institutional design shapes learning conditions
- Transition: Secondary (flexible, teacher-centered) → Tertiary (formal, student-responsible)
- Challenge: Didactical contract changes; what was acceptable explanation shifts to requiring rigorous proof
- Issue: Institutions rarely explicitly teach these norm shifts

### Why Multidimensional Matters
Single-perspective analyses mislead:
- **Cognitive-only**: "Students need better high school mathematics"—ignores that social/institutional norms differ
- **Social-only**: "Students need to learn proof culture"—ignores cognitive tools needed
- **Institutional-only**: "Need better curricula"—ignores cognitive development required

**Effective STT support requires interventions across all three dimensions simultaneously.**
