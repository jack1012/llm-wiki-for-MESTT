# Geisler 2023 - Mid-term Prediction Gold Standard

## The Concept
Geisler (2023) argues that mid-semester affective data is the "gold standard" for identifying students at risk of dropout, significantly outperforming entry-level data.

## Evidence
- **Entry-level (Pre-test)**: Many students enter with high enthusiasm and inflated self-concepts, which masks future struggle.
- **Mid-term (Week 10-12)**: By this point, students have experienced the "reality shock" of university lectures and homework. Their affective state at this point reflects their actual adjustment to the **[[Secondary-to-Tertiary Transition (STT)]]**.
- **Result**: The correlation between week 10 affective scores and end-of-semester dropout was much higher than week 1 scores.

## Implication for Institutions
Waiting until end-of-semester results to identify at-risk students is too late. Institutions should implement **affective screening** around the mid-term to offer targeted support to those whose self-concept has collapsed.
