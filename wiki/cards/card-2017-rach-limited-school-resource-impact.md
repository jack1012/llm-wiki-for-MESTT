# Rach 2017 - Limited School Resource Impact

## The Concept
Rach and Heinze (2017) found that traditional success indicators in school (General math grade, overall GPA) have surprisingly little predictive power for university mathematics success.

## Key Findings
- **Correlation Decay**: While school grades correlate highly with each other, their correlation with university exam results is marginal.
- **Why?**: The nature of "success" changes. In school, success is often tied to following teacher instructions and procedural accuracy. At university, it requires self-regulated abstraction.
- **The "High Achiever" Trap**: Students who relied on high secondary performance without developing deep conceptual strategies are most at risk of "unexpected" failure.

## Implication
Universities should not rely on school grades for admission or streaming. Instead, they need to identify students capable of **[[Scientific Mathematical Knowledge|scientific thinking]]**.
