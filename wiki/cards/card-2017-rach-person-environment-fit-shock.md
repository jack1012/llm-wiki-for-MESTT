# Rach 2017 - Person-Environment Fit Shock

## The Concept
Rach (2017) applies the **Person-Environment Fit (P-E Fit)** theory to explain why previously successful students often experience a "crisis" at the STT.

## Key Theoretical Insights
- **Incongruence**: The transition crisis is not caused by a lack of student ability (the "Person" is often high-achieving) but by the sudden **incongruence** between the school environment (guided, applied, supportive) and the university environment (autonomous, abstract, impersonal).
- **Secondary Environment**: High environmental support + procedural demands.
- **Tertiary Environment**: Low environmental support + formal/structural demands.
- **Result**: The "fit" that made the student successful in school is shattered, requiring a complete restructuring of the student's learning habits and self-concept.

## Conclusion
Retention is an "interactive fit" problem (echoing **[[Vincent Tinto|Tinto 1973]]**), but with a specific focus on the mismatch in **mathematical standards**.
