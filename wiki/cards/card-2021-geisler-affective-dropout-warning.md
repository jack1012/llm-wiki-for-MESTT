# Geisler 2021 - Affective Dropout Warning

## The Concept
Geisler (2021) suggests that we can predict dropout risk earlier by monitoring the **Affective Trajectory** of students.

## The Warning Signs
1.  **Steep Interest Decline**: Between Week 1 and Week 6, a sharp drop in interest suggests the student has realized they do not "fit" the university math culture.
2.  **Self-concept Erosion**: When a student's perceived competence collapses relative to their peers, they often stop attending tutorials, leading to invisible dropout.
3.  **Low Mid-term Calibration**: Students whose mid-term self-evaluations are significantly lower than their entry beliefs are at the highest risk.

## Practice
Using brief "Pulse Surveys" in Week 4-6 on interest and satisfaction can identify at-risk students *before* they fail the mid-term, providing a window for advising.
