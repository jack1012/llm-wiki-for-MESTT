# Geisler 2021 - Interest-Persistence Link

## The Concept
Geisler (2021) discovered a functional dissociation between different success metrics during the transition.

## The Finding
- **Interest $\rightarrow$ Persistence**: A student's **Interest** in university-level mathematics is the strongest predictor of whether they will show up to the exam (Retention). If interest is lost, they drop out *before* the assessment.
- **Competence $\rightarrow$ Grade**: A student's **Self-concept and Prior Knowledge** are the strongest predictors of the actual score they achieve *if* they stay.

## Significance
Many "talented" students (high competence) drop out not because they *can't* do the math, but because they no longer find it *interesting* or valuable (low interest). Retention interventions should therefore focus on "Interest-generation" rather than just "Remedial-practice."
