## 核心概念 (Core Concept: English Body)
> 一卡一概念：一張卡片只談一個概念，避免混雜。

- **Problem**: How do initial affective variables like "interest" and "self-concept" predict physical attendance in university lectures?
- **Method**: Quantitative study tracking attendance rates and correlating them with entry-point affective scores.
- **Conclusion**: **Initial Mathematical Interest** is a strong predictor of persistence in attending lectures. Students who enter with higher intrinsic interest are more likely to show up even when the lectures become difficult. However, interest alone does not guarantee performance; it primarily predicts the willingness to stay in the learning environment.

This highlights that "Showing Up" is an affective decision. When interest wanes, disappearance from the physical classroom is the first step toward actual dropout.
