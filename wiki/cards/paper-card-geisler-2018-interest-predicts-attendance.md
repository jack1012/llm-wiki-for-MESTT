- **Problem**: 25% of students do not even attend the final exam, indicating early dropout. We need to know what affective variable keeps them in the seat.
- **Method**: Logistic regression comparing multiple variables against the binary outcome of exam attendance.
- **Conclusion**: Interest in mathematics is the single most significant predictor of attendance. While self-efficacy or competence were helpful for marks, they did not predict whether a student showed up. Students with low interest had a much higher probability of giving up before the assessment phase.

This suggests that persistence in the mathematics program is driven more by the intrinsic value and "fun" of the subject than by the student's belief in their own ability (self-concept). If the "fire" of interest is lost during the transition, students are likely to withdraw before they even test their capabilities.
