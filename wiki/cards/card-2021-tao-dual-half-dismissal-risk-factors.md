## Key Insight
Using a Cox proportional hazard model on institutional data, Tao (2021) identifies several critical risk factors for university dismissal in Taiwan under the "Dual Half" rule:
- **Gender**: Males have a significantly higher hazard of dismissal (70% higher than females).
- **Entry Path**: Transfer students and those returning from leave (resumption) are at higher risk.
- **Financial Status**: Students with student loans are more vulnerable.
- **Academic Baseline**: Lower GSAT (學測) scores correlate with higher dismissal risk.

**STT Implication**: These factors suggest that the transition is not just a cognitive challenge but is mediated by socio-economic and demographic variables. Schools should target "at-risk" profiles early in the first year.
