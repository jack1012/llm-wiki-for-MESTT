# Biza 2016 - Pedagogical Orientations Taxonomy

## The Concept
Biza et al. (2016) found that university lecturers respond to the "transition gap" based on four distinct orientations.

## The Taxonomy
1.  **Jump-oriented**: Views the gap as a necessary formal leap. Focuses on rigor and "tough love"; expects students to adapt quickly or fail.
2.  **Enculturation-oriented**: Sees transition as a socialization process. Focuses on introducing students to the *rituals* and *language* of mathematicians.
3.  **Diversity-oriented**: Recognizes that students have very different backgrounds. Adapts teaching to meet diverse needs (e.g., differentiated resources).
4.  **Cross-community-oriented**: Believes the gap must be bridged through dialogue with high school teachers to align curricula.

## Significance
A math department's "culture" is determined by which of these orientations is dominant. Students may experience greater shock if their school was "Diversity-oriented" and their university is "Jump-oriented."
