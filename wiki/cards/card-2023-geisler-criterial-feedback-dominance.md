# Geisler 2023 - Criterial Feedback Dominance

## The Concept
Geisler (2023) identified that **Criterial Feedback** (feedback based on objective correctness and formal standards) is the most powerful trigger for changes in a student's mathematical self-concept.

## Findings
- **High Sensitivity**: Students are most vulnerable to self-concept decline when they receive their graded homework. 
- **The "Shock" Mechanism**: Because university standards of "correctness" (rigor, notation, logic) are higher than school standards, students who were used to being "right" in school often receive poor feedback, causing a direct hit to their self-identity.
- **Situation**: Most impactful situations occurred during individual work on homework or when reviewing returned assignments.

## Pedagogical Implication
Feedback should be designed to be **formative** rather than just **criterial** in the early weeks of STT to prevent students from equating "correcting my homework" with "failing as a mathematician."
