# Liston 2007 - Service Math Paradox

## The Concept
Liston (2007) highlights a specific difficulty in **Service Mathematics** (math for non-math majors like Bio or Eng).

## The Paradox
1.  **Low Identity**: These students do not identify as "mathematicians." They view math as a "tool" they need to pass.
2.  **High Stakes**: However, their success in their primary degree (e.g., Engineering) is heavily dependent on passing difficult math modules.
3.  **The Tension**: Because they lack interest in abstract theory but are forced into rigorous university math environments, their motivation is purely extrinsic. This makes them more susceptible to the **[[Mathematical Crisis]]** than math majors.

## Significance
Teaching for "service" students requires a different pedagogical contract—one that emphasizes the **Utility** of the abstract concepts they are learning.
