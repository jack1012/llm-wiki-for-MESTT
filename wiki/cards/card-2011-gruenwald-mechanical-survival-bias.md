# Gruenwald 2011 - Mechanical Survival Bias

## The Concept
Gruenwald (2011) describes a phenomenon where students "survive" the first few weeks of university math using only secondary-school **Procedural Knowledge**, creating a false sense of security.

## The Mechanism
1.  **Masking**: The first-year syllabus often overlaps with advanced secondary topics (e.g., limits, basic derivatives).
2.  **Mechanical Execution**: Students apply school algorithms to pass initial homework.
3.  **The Wall**: When the course shifts to **[[Axiomatic-Formal World|Formal Rigor]]** and non-standard problems, the mechanical strategies fail instantly.
4.  **The Shock**: Because the student felt "successful" for the first month, the subsequent failure is experienced as an inexplicable **[[Mathematical Crisis]]**.

## Pedagogical Lesson
University staff must introduce "non-algorithmic" conceptual tasks in Week 1 to shatter this survival bias early and signal that a new way of thinking is required.
