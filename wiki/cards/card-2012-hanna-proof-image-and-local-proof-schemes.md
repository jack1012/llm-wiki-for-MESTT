## Key Insight
Building on Harel & Sowder's (1998) taxonomy, this chapter identifies three types of student **Proof Schemes**:
1. **External Conviction Schemes**: Student relies on authority (teacher says so) or ritual (algebraic manipulation).
2. **Empirical Schemes**: Student convinces through examples (inductive reasoning).
3. **Deductive/Analytical Schemes**: Student uses formal logical argument.

**STT Implication**: School mathematics predominantly trains students in *External* and *Empirical* schemes. University mathematics demands an immediate and complete shift to *Deductive* schemes, creating a cognitive crisis. This mirrors Tall's (1998) "procept" theory.
