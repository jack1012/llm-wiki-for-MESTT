# Symonds 2015 - At-Risk Disconnect

## The Concept
Symonds (2015) identifies the **At-Risk Disconnect** (or the Support Paradox): those students who would benefit most from mathematics support are often the least likely to seek it.

## Key Insights
- **Inverted Usage**: Usage data shows that "B" or "C" grade students often use support centers to reach "A" level (Enhancement), while failing students (the "Target" at-risk group) remain invisible to support services.
- **Cognitive Load & Avoidance**: Struggling students often feel overwhelmed; seeking help adds a new "task" to their already saturated cognitive load, leading to avoidance behavior.
- **Institutional Estrangement**: Students with low self-efficacy feel they "don't belong" in the support center, fearing they will be judged for their lack of basic knowledge.

## Conclusion
Voluntary "drop-in" models are insufficient for student persistence. Effective transition support must be **Proactive** and **Invisible** (embedded into the curriculum).
