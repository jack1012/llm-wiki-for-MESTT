# Gruenwald 2004 - Bridging Courses Limitations

## The Problem
Many university "bridging" or "remedial" courses focus on filling **content gaps** (e.g., re-teaching calculus rules) rather than addressing the **cognitive/epistemological gap**.

## Key Critique
- **Symptoms vs. Root Causes**: Reteaching content addresses the symptoms of failure but not the root cause (the procedural mindset).
- **Misaligned Goals**: If a bridging course uses the same "procedural" teaching style as secondary school, it only reinforces the behavior that causes failure in advanced university courses.

## Recommendation
Interventions should explicitly target **Mathematical Maturity**. Students need induction into the *culture* and *language* of university mathematics, not just more practice with derivatives.
