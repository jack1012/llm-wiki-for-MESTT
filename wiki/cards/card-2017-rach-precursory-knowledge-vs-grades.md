# Rach 2017 - Precursory Knowledge vs. Grades

## The Concept
Rach (2017) distinguishes between **General Mathematical Knowledge** (GPA/Grades) and **Specific Precursory Knowledge** (readiness for university abstraction).

## Key Findings
- **Predictive Dominance**: Specific precursory knowledge—measured by testing concepts like formal logic, proof-readiness, and abstract reasoning—is the single strongest predictor of 1st-semester success.
- **Scientific Standard**: University math expects students to participate in a "scientific" discourse where definitions and axioms are the objects of study, not just tools for computation.
- **The Gap**: Students may have an 'A' grade in school math but score low on "scientific" precursory tests because they lack experience with formal rigor.

## Pedagogical Implication
Transition support should focus on transitioning students from "school math" to "scientific math" as early as possible (the **Pre-Transition** phase).
