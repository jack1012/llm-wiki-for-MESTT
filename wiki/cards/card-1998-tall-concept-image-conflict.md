# Tall 1998 - Concept Image Conflict

## The Concept
A hallmark of David Tall's research is the distinction between a student's **Concept Image** and the formal **Concept Definition**.

## Definitions
- **Concept Image**: The idiosyncratic set of mental pictures, examples, and non-examples a student associates with a name (e.g., "Function" might evoke a picture of a curvy line).
- **Concept Definition**: The formal, linguistically rigorous definition (e.g., the set-theoretic definition of a mapping).

## Conflict in STT
In school, students survive using only their **Concept Image**. In university, they are tested on their ability to deduce from the **Concept Definition**. When the image and definition conflict (e.g., a "constant function" is still a function but doesn't look like a "curvy line"), the student experiences cognitive anxiety and error.
