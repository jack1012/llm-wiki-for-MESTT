# Geisler 2015 - Static vs. Dynamic Beliefs

## The Concept
Geisler (2015) categorizes student beliefs about mathematics into two types and links them to transition success.

## The Types
1.  **Static Beliefs (靜態信念)**: Mathematics is seen as a finished product—a collection of formulas, rules, and facts to be memorized and applied.
    - *Impact*: Higher risk of dropout when "formulas" no longer explain the "proofs."
2.  **Dynamic Beliefs (動態信念)**: Mathematics is seen as a process, an evolving discipline of discovery and logical creation.
    - *Impact*: Better persistence and higher satisfaction, as the student is prepared for the "discovery" nature of university math.

## Critical Finding
During the first semester, students' **Dynamic Beliefs** often decline because the massive volume of new content forces them back into a "survival/static" mode. Preventing this decline is a key goal for successful STT.
