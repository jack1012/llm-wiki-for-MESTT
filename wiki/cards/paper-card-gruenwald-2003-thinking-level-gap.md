- **Problem**: 72% of university lecturers perceive a "major jump" in the level of abstract thinking required. High school mathematics is characterized as mechanical and situational, focusing on "recipes" for standard problems.
- **Method**: Analysis of survey responses from 63 international lecturers regarding the nature of mathematics at different educational levels.
- **Conclusion**: University mathematics represents a bifurcation from technical/computational math towards formal/axiomatic math. The school emphasis on "getting an answer" through symbol manipulation leaves students unprepared for the precision and proof-oriented nature of tertiary studies.

University lecturers point out that "high-attaining" school students often lack fundamental notions because school assessment culture prioritizes passing exams over deep understanding. This creates a "conceptual obstacle" where students' prior successful strategies (rote algorithms) become liabilities in a formal deductive environment.
