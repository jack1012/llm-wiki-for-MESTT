# Lovric 2011 - Rite of Passage Model

## The Concept
Clark & Lovric (2011) propose viewing the secondary-tertiary transition not as a "problem to solve," but as a **Rite of Passage**—a necessary, transformative period of growth.

## The Three Stages
1.  **Separation**: Students leave behind their identity as "senior high school experts." This is marked by the loss of direct teacher support and familiar grading systems.
2.  **Liminality (The Threshold)**: The "in-between" state. Students feel they know nothing; the old rules don't work, and the new ones aren't clear yet. This is the period of highest risk and anxiety.
3.  **Incorporation**: The student successfully adopts the discursive and social rules of the university. They "become" a member of the mathematical community.

## Significance
Viewing STT as a "rite" helps institutions recognize that **Confusion** is a natural and necessary part of the process, rather than a sign of failure. The goal is to shepherd students *through* the liminal stage.
