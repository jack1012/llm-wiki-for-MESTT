# Tall 1998 - Proceptual Divide Analysis

## The Concept
David Tall identifies a **Proceptual Divide** as a major cause of failure in the transition to university mathematics.

## Definition of terms
- **Process**: The action (e.g., $3 + 2$ is an action to count).
- **Object**: The result (e.g., $5$ is a mental object).
- **Procept**: A symbol that can evoke both the process and the object.

## The Divide
- **Lower Achievers**: Remain trapped in **Procedural Thinking**. They see math as a series of steps to follow. As complexity increases (e.g., differentials), their mental load becomes too heavy.
- **Higher Achievers**: Develop **Proceptual Thinking**. They can compress a complex process into a single mental object.
- **In STT**: Students who were successful in school using pure procedures (e.g., "follow the recipe for integration") hit a wall in university where symbols represent abstract structures (objects) that cannot be "computed" away.
