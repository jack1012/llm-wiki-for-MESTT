## Key Insight
Using Boekaerts' dual processing model, students' self-regulation is driven by their "appraisal" of tasks. If a task is appraised as congruent with growth goals, students engage in learning; if appraised as a threat to well-being (e.g., too difficult/irrelevant), regulation shifts to "ego-protection" or disengagement.
