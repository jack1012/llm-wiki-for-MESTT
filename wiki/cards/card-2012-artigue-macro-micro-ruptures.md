# Artigue 2012 - Macro-Micro Ruptures

## The Concept
Artigue (2012) categorizes transition difficulties into two scales of rupture.

## Scales
1.  **Macro-ruptures (тиеУДђТќиУБѓ)**: The obvious, large-scale changes.
    - Examples: Moving from small classes to 500-person halls; new social independence; shift in the entire assessment system.
2.  **Micro-ruptures (тЙ«УДђТќиУБѓ)**: The subtle, often invisible changes within mathematical tasks.
    - Examples: The shift in **Pace** (more math delivered faster); the increased **Task Diversity** (problems look different every session); the demand for **Autonomy** (finding your own way to solve a proof).

## The Danger
Institutions often focus on fixing Macro-ruptures (mentoring, orientation) but ignore the Micro-ruptures. However, it is the accumulation of these tiny, daily cognitive breaks that causes students to eventually feel "Lost in Transition."
