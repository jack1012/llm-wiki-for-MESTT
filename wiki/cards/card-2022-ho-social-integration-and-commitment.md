## Key Insight
Ho (2022) emphasizes that **Institutional Commitment** (the desire to stay at a specific school) is heavily dependent on **Social Integration** (peer and teacher interaction). 

- **The Trade-off**: High social integration can compensate for low academic performance, keeping a student in the system. However, high academic performance cannot always compensate for social isolation.
- **Pedagogical Re-socialization**: Successful students are those who successfully "re-socialize" into the institution's social norms, shifting from their "school self" to an "institutional self."

## STT Implication
During the transition, students lose their existing social capital (high school friends/teachers). The speed at which they can build new "social capital" at university determines their institutional commitment and, ultimately, their persistence.
