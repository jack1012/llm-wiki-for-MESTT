## Key Insight
Drawing on Tinto's application of **Durkheim's Theory of Suicide**, Ho (2022) frames student dropout not as a failure of ability, but as a voluntary or involuntary departure from a social system where the individual no longer finds "moral integration" or "rewarding interaction."

**STT Implication**: The "shock" of transition is often a social one. If a student's values or interaction patterns do not match the new "university society," they experience a form of institutional alienation. This "social death" precedes the physical act of dropping out.
