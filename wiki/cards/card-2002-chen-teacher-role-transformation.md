## Key Insight
A major barrier to successful curriculum articulation is the **Teacher's Mindset**. Chen (2002) argues that teachers must transition from "I am the king in the classroom" (authority-centered) to "Facilitator of knowledge construction" (learner-centered).

- **Internal Conflict**: Teachers often resist reform because it challenges their professional identity and requires new skills (e.g., guiding discussion instead of lecturing).
- **The "Facilitator" Role**: Teachers should learn *with* the students, acknowledging that they are also "lifelong learners" in a changing educational landscape.

## STT Implication
The student's transition experience is mediated by the teacher's approach. A teacher who refuses to bridge the gap (by refusing to acknowledge the change in junior high standards) exacerbates the student's transition shock. Successful STT requires teachers to actively "scaffold" the transition.
