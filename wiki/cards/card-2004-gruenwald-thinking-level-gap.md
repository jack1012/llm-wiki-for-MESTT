# Gruenwald 2004 - Thinking Level Gap

## The Concept
University lecturers identify a mismatch between what students *can* do and what they *understand*. Students arrive from secondary school with high grades but a **procedural mindset**.

## Key Observations
- **Surface Success**: Students solve complex integrals or derivatives using memorized rules but cannot explain when or why the rules apply.
- **The "Procedural Conspiracy"**: Secondary education often rewards the reproduction of algorithms, creating a false sense of preparedness.
- **University Reality**: Immediate demand for **abstract thinking** and **rigorous justification**.

## Implications for STT
If lecturers assume conceptual depth is already present, they teach at a level that bypasses the student's actual cognitive state, leading to immediate frustration and failure in the first semester.
