# Winslow 2009 - Ontological Foreground

## The Concept
Winsløw (2009) uses the term **Ontological Foreground** to explain why different theories in mathematics education often seem to disagree.

## The Theory
- No theory can model the entire complexity of the classroom with equal depth.
- Every theory "foregrounds" one component (G, O, or A) while "backgrounding" the others.
- **Example**: Constructivism foregrounds the **G (Group/Individual)**, seeing math through the lens of human cognition. ATD (Anthropological Theory) foregrounds the **O (Organization)**, seeing math as a set of institutional praxeologies.
- **Conflict?**: The "conflict" is often just a result of looking at different parts of the same **[[Epistemic System (ES)]]**.

## Conclusion
Understanding a theory's foreground allows researchers to "network" them—using one theory to explain the organization and another to explain the human experience.
