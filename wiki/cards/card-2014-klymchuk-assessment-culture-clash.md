# Klymchuk 2014 - Assessment Culture Clash

## The Concept
Klymchuk (2014) highlights how the **Culture of Assessment** in schools directly sabotages the transition to the university exam environment.

## The Clash
- **School Culture (NCEA/Internal)**: Assessment is often bite-sized, predictable, and allows for multiple "re-submissions." This fosters a "recipe-based" approach where students master specific "standard-aligned" tasks.
- **University Culture (High-Stakes Exams)**: Assessment involves summative, one-shot exams covering a vast, integrated syllabus. The focus is on **Deep Transfer** and logic.

## Feedback Shock
When students enter university, the lack of constant, low-stakes "standards-based" feedback (the **[[Criterial Feedback]]** shock) leads to a rapid decline in **[[Mathematical Self-concept|self-concept]]**, as they no longer have "recipes" to guarantee success.
