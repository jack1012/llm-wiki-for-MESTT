# Biza 2016 - Lecturer Blame Narratives

## The Concept
Biza et al. (2016) explore how lecturers construct narratives to explain the transition gap.

## Common Narratives
- **Students are Lazier**: The view that modern students don't have the work ethic of previous generations.
- **Schools are Failing**: Blaming secondary teachers for focused too much on "teaching to the test" (Proceduralism).
- **The "Good Old Days"**: A nostalgic view of a time when students arrived with "perfect" algebra skills.

## The Problem
These narratives often prevent lecturers from reflecting on their own **Pedagogical Practices**. By blaming external factors (students/schools), they justify not changing their teaching methods to accommodate the shifting demographics of higher education.
