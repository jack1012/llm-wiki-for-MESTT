# Rach 2024 - Interest-Effort Interaction

## The Concept
Rach (2024) discovered that the behavioral effect of **Profession-Related Tasks (PRTs)** depends on the student's initial **interest in school mathematics**.

## Findings
- **High Initial Interest**: Students who entered university loving "school math" showed significantly increased **effort** when presented with PRTs. The tasks reinforced their passion by proving it was relevant to their career.
- **Low Initial Interest**: For students who already struggled with or disliked school math, PRTs increased the perceived *value* but didn't necessarily result in more *effort*.

## Implication
PRTs are highly effective at "keeping the engaged students engaged." They are a retention tool for those who are most at risk of losing their passion due to the abstract nature of university courses.
