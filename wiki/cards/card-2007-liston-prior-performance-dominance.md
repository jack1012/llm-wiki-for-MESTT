# Liston 2007 - Prior Performance Dominance

## The Finding
Liston (2007) finds that in the Irish context, the **Leaving Certificate** (final secondary exam) result remains the single strongest predictor of university performance.

## The Analysis
- **The Success Anchor**: Students with "Honors" math results in school possess a baseline of fluency that allows them to absorb university content faster.
- **The Risk Factor**: Students entering with "Ordinary level" or lower grades face a "Double Challenge": they must learn new university concepts *while* simultaneously remediation their secondary school gaps.
- **Cognitive Load**: The effort required for remediation takes mental energy away from adapting to **[[Advanced Mathematical Thinking]]**, leading to a higher failure rate.

## Conclusion
While affective factors (like interest) decide if a student *stays*, prior performance often decides if they *pass*.
