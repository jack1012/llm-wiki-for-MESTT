# Geisler 2023 - Affect Trajectory and Dropout

## The Concept
Geisler (2023) demonstrates that dropout is not just predicted by low starting points, but by the **rate of decline** in affective variables during the first semester.

## Key Findings
- **The Gap**: Students who persist maintain relatively stable interest and self-concept. Dropout students experience a rapid "crash" within the first 10-12 weeks.
- **Variable Sensitivity**: **Mathematical Self-concept** showed a more pronounced decline than general interest among dropout candidates.
- **Timing**: Mid-semester measurements were significantly more accurate at predicting actual dropout than entry-level measurements.

## Significance
This highlights that the "transition crisis" is a process that unfolds over time. Early interventions (before week 10) are critical to arrest the decline in self-confidence before it leads to withdrawal.
