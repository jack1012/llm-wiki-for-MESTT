# Card: {{title}}

## 💎 核心概念 (Core Concept: English Body)
> 一卡一概念：比較「靜態工具箱觀」與「動態過程觀」對轉銜成效的預測。

- **Problem**: Does the way a student defines "what math is" influence their actual performance and persistence in university mathematics?
- **Method**: Longitudinal regression study (N=602) testing static beliefs (math as a collection of rules) and dynamic beliefs (math as creative and applicable).
- **Conclusion**: Dynamic beliefs are associated with deep learning approaches and higher satisfaction. Crucially, they predict better achievement in real analysis exams and lower dropout risk. Static beliefs, however, are associated with surface learning and act as a negative predictor of satisfaction. By the middle of the semester, dynamic beliefs become the strongest predictor of whether a student will stay in the program.

The results highlight that students who view math as a "vivid discipline involving creativity" are more resilient when facing the abstraction shock of university lectures. Those who hold onto the "toolbox belief" feel increasingly dissatisfied as university math moves away from mechanical calculation.

## 🧠 個人思考與關聯 (Reflection)
- **啟發與想法**: 這說明了「信念」是行動的過濾器。如果學生認為數學只是公式，當他們看到 epsilon-delta 這種「沒有公式可帶」的東西時，他們的內在系統就會崩潰。
- **知識缺口**: 研究發現動態信念在第一學期中會略微下降，這是因為大學教學過於形式化導致的嗎？如何維持學生的動態信念？
- **評論**: 這呼應了 Schoenfeld (1985) 的觀點。這告訴我們，大學端在教知識的同時，也必須改變學生的觀念，否則就是在「用舊地圖走新路」。
