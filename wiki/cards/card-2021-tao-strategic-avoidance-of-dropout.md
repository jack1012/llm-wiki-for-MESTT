## Key Insight
Tao (2021) discovers that the threat of dismissal triggers "Strategic Avoidance" behaviors in students, which can be viewed as a form of **Academic Risk Management**:
1. **Time Distortion**: Students shift effort away from difficult/risky courses to ensure passing "safe" ones.
2. **Withdrawal Reduction**: Paradoxically, students at risk withdraw from *fewer* courses because they need to reach a minimum credit threshold to avoid the "Half-fail" count.
3. **Seeking Mercy**: There is a clustering of grades at exactly 60 points, suggesting students actively negotiate for passing grades with instructors to survive the system.

**STT Implication**: Rigid dismissal policies may prioritize "system survival" over "authentic learning." Students become "grade-seekers" rather than "knowledge-seekers" during the critical transition period.
