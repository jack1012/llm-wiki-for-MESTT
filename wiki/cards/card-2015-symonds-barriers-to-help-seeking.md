# Symonds 2015 - Barriers to Help-seeking

## The Concept
Symonds (2015) identifies several psychological and physical **Barriers** that prevent freshmen from utilizing mathematics support during the transition.

## The Barriers
1.  **Stigma of Weakness**: Students worry that entering a support center is an admission of failure or "not being university material."
2.  **Social Anxiety**: The fear of asking a "stupid question" in front of peers or lecturers.
3.  **Low Self-efficacy**: "Even if I go, I won't understand it." This learned helplessness prevents the first step.
4.  **Logistical Friction**: If the center is located in a different building or has limited hours, students in low-motivation states won't make the effort.

## Design Strategy
To overcome these, MSCs should be rebranded as **"Learning Commons"** where all levels of students work together, reducing the shame of being "remedial."
