# Wasserman 2023 - PMP Pedagogical Practice

## Definition
**Pedagogical Mathematical Practices (PMPs)** are practices that are both fundamentally mathematical (used by professional mathematicians) and deeply pedagogical (useful for teaching).

## Examples
- **Defining**: Not just stating a definition, but the practice of *negotiating* and *refining* a definition to include/exclude cases—a core skill for teaching new concepts.
- **Justifying**: Using logic rather than authority to convince others of a truth.
- **Handling Counter-examples**: Systematically checking the boundaries of a theorem.

## Impact on STT
By focusing on PMPs, university courses help teachers see that university math is not just "content to memorize" but a "way of thinking" that they can model for their future students.
