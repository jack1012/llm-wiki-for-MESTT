# Di Martino 2016 - First-time Failure Trigger

## The Concept
Di Martino (2016) highlights the unique psychological impact of a student’s **First-time Failure** in mathematics during the transition.

## Key Findings
- **Lack of Coping Mechanisms**: Students who have never failed in school have not developed "resilience strategies" for mathematics.
- **The Attribution Trap**: Because they have always succeeded through "natural ability," they attribute their first failure at university also to "natural ability" (or lack thereof), rather than a lack of new study methods.
- **Critical Window**: The weeks immediately following the first failed mid-term or assignment are the "danger zone" where the **[[Self-Concept Decline]]** is most acute.

## Practical Tip
Institutions should provide **Attribution Retraining** sessions after the first round of assessments to help students reframe failure as a "lack of effective university strategies" rather than a "lack of mathematical brain."
