# Liston 2007 - Mathematical Fragility Index

## The Concept
Liston (2007) describes the **Mathematical Fragility** (lack of resilience) common in first-year university students.

## The Observation
- **The Five-Minute Wall**: In interviews, students admit that if they cannot recall the correct procedure for a problem within five minutes, they stop. 
- **Algorithmic Dependence**: Students view math as a "set of recipes." If the recipe is forgotten or doesn't fit the problem exactly, they feel they have no "tools" to independently investigate.
- **Consequence**: This leads to a high rate of assignment abandonment and exam panic when encountering non-standard university questions.

## Educational Response
Transition courses must explicitly teach **Problem-solving Strategies** (Pólya's style) to give students tools to continue working when their memory of specific algorithms fails.
