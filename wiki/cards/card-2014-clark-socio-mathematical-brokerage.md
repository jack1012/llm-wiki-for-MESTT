# Clark 2014 - Socio-mathematical Brokerage

## The Concept
To bridge the transition, Clark (2014) advocates for lecturers to act as **Socio-mathematical Brokers**.

## The Role
- **Brokerage**: A broker is someone who belongs to two different cultures and helps translate between them. 
- **Action**: Instead of just lecturing on "content," the broker explicitly discusses the **[[Didactical Contract]]**.
- **Examples**:
    - "In school, you were allowed to say X, but here, we require Y because of Z."
    - "I am giving you this difficult task not to fail you, but to show you how a mathematician thinks through confusion."

## Goal
To reduce the **[[Culture Shock (Mathematical)]]** by making the implicit rules of the university explicit, thus lowering the cognitive load of the transition.
