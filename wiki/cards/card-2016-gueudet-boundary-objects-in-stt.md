# Gueudet 2016 - Boundary Objects in STT

## The Concept
Drawing from Star & Griesemer, the ICME survey (Gueudet, 2016) explores how **Boundary Objects** can facilitate the secondary-tertiary transition.

## Definition
A **Boundary Object** is a tool or task that is used in both contexts (school and university) but may have different meanings or uses in each. It acts as a bridge.

## Examples in STT
- **Calculus Problems**: A problem can be solved procedurally (school) or axiomatically (university). Discussing these two ways makes the boundary visible.
- **Diagnostic Manuals**: Symbols or definitions used at both levels but with different rigor demands.
- **Graphic Calculators**: Tools that migrate between school and university but serve different epistemic functions.

## Goal
To use these objects to create "Brokerage" between the two cultures, allowing students to map their existing school knowledge onto the new university expectations.
