# Gruenwald 2011 - Recommendations for Reduction

## The Strategy
Based on an international survey of lecturers, Gruenwald et al. (2011) propose three pillars to reduce the STT gap.

## The Pillars
1.  **Macro-Level (Institutional)**: Establish mandatory dialogue forums between secondary math leads and university first-year coordinators to align the **[[Didactical Contract]]**.
2.  **Meso-Level (Curricular)**: Integration of **[[Mathematical Modelling]]** as a core thread throughout calculus, not just as isolated "word problems" at the end of chapters.
3.  **Micro-Level (Pedagogical)**: Explicitly teaching **Mathematical Language** (quantifiers, symbols) as a "secondary language," recognizing that the notation is a barrier in itself.

## Goal
Moving from a "Filtering" model (letting students fail) to a "Structured Transition" model where the cognitive breaks are explicitly acknowledged and coached.
