# Geisler 2015 - Mid-term Calibration Effect

## The Concept
Geisler (2015) identified that student beliefs and self-concepts measured **in the middle of the first semester** are significantly more accurate predictors of final success than those measured at the start (Entry).

## Why?
- **Entry Naivety**: At the start, many students respond based on their high school experience ("I am good at math"). They haven't encountered university-level rigor yet.
- **The Calibration**: By mid-term, students have received feedback (assignments, first quizzes) and have experienced the **[[Culture Shock (Mathematical)]]**.
- **Realized Fit**: Mid-term data reflects the student's *actual* fit with the university environment, making it the "Gold Standard" window for identifying students at risk of dropping out.

## Practice
Interventions should be timed shortly after the first mid-term feedback to catch students whose self-concept has just "calibrated" downwards.
