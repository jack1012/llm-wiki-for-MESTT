# Di Martino 2016 - Affective-Identity Crisis

## The Concept
Di Martino (2016) identifies that the STT transition is often an **Identity Crisis** masquerading as an academic problem.

## The Mechanism
1.  **Fixed Identity**: High-achieving secondary students often have a fixed identity: "I am the one who is good at math."
2.  **The Collision**: At university, they encounter abstract rigor that their previous "embodied" or "procedural" skills cannot solve.
3.  **The Collapse**: When they fail, it isn't just a "bad grade"; it is a refutation of their identity. If "I am the one who is good at math" and I am failing, then "I am no longer myself."
4.  **Result**: Emotional paralysis and a high risk of dropout, as the student tries to "protect" their remaining self-worth by distancing themselves from math.
