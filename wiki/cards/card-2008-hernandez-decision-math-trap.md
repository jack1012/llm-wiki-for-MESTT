# Hernandez-Martinez 2008 - Decision Math Trap

## The Concept
Hernandez-Martinez (2008) warns that curricula designed to make transition "easier" (such as "Decision Mathematics" in the UK) can actually damage a student's **Mathematical Identity**.

## The Mechanism
1.  **Over-Proceduralization**: These curricula often rely on algorithmic, mark-earning strategies rather than abstract logic.
2.  **False Security**: Students feel they are "good at math" because they follow the recipes, but they are not developing the **[[Mathematical Maturity]]** needed for university.
3.  **Identity Fragility**: When these students reach higher-level courses where recipes fail, their identity as a "capable mathematician" collapses because it was built on a procedural foundation.

## Conclusion
"Bridging" by simplifying content can lead to a more severe secondary shock (the **[[Double Discontinuity]]**).
