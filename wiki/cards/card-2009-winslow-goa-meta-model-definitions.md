# Winslow 2009 - GOA Meta-model Definitions

## The Concept
Winsløw (2009) argues that any theory in mathematics education must account for three elements: **Groups (G)**, **Organizations (O)**, and **Artefacts (A)**.

## The Components
- **G (Groups/Humans)**: The actors. Includes students, teachers, institutions, and their social relationships.
- **O (Organizations)**: The knowledge structure. Includes mathematical praxeologies, didactic organizations, and curriculum.
- **A (Artefacts)**: The mediators. Includes language, symbols, calculators, and discursive tools used to bridge G and O.

## Importance
By identifying these three universal components, researchers can see where a theory is strong (e.g., ATD is very strong in 'O') and where it might be weak (e.g., 'G' - individual student psychology), facilitating a more complete research design.
