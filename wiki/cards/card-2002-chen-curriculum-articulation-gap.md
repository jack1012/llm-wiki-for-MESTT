## Key Insight
Chen (2002) identifies a systemic "rupture" in Taiwan's mathematics education caused by mismatched pedagogical philosophies. While the Grade 1-9 curriculum (Junior High) moved toward **informal, life-oriented mathematics**, the Grade 10-12 curriculum (Senior High) remained anchored in **formal, deductive, and abstract mathematics**.

- **The Rupture**: Students enter high school with a "Constructivist" mindset but are suddenly expected to perform "Traditional" formal proofs and complex algebraic manipulations (e.g., sequences, polynomials) that were previously removed or simplified in junior high.
- **Cognitive Impact**: This creates a "Stage-Mismatch" where students who haven't mastered symbolic logic are forced into high-level abstraction.

## STT Implication
Curriculum transition is a major component of the STT. When the "vertical articulation" (縱向銜接) between educational levels is broken, it results in a sudden drop in student self-efficacy and a spike in failure rates in Grade 10 (Senior High 1).
