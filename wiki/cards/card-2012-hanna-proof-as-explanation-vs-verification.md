## Key Insight
Hanna (2000, 2012) distinguishes between two fundamentally different functions of proof:
- **Proof that proves (Verification)**: Establishes the truth of a statement. This is the primary function in formal university mathematics.
- **Proof that explains (Explanation)**: Reveals *why* a statement is true. This is what students find meaningful and what school mathematics primarily offers.

**STT Implication**: The shift from school (explanation-centred) to university (verification-centred) proof culture is a primary cause of the "proof shock" (Hanna, 1995). Students who only know "why proofs work" struggle when they must construct "airtight logical chains" from axioms.
