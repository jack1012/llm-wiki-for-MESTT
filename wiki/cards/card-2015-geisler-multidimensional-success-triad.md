# Geisler 2015 - Multidimensional Success Triad

## The Concept
Geisler (2015) argues that evaluating "success" in university mathematics based solely on exam grades is flawed. He proposes a **Three-Dimensional Model**:

## The Triad
1.  **Achievement (學業成就)**: Passing exams and understanding the formal content. (The traditional metric)
2.  **Satisfaction (學習滿意度)**: The student’s affective wellbeing and feeling that they chose the right field. A student can pass but still be deeply dissatisfied.
3.  **Retention (留存/不退學)**: The decision to continue the study. Satisfaction and Achievement combined predict retention.

## Significance
This model explains high-achieving dropouts—students who pass the exams but find the university math "culture" so alienating (low satisfaction) that they leave the program anyway.
