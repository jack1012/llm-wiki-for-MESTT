# Guzman 1998 - Triple Taxonomy of Difficulties

## The Concept
Guzman et al. (1998) established the "Standard Model" for categorizing the reasons students fail during the math transition.

## The Taxonomy
1.  **Epistemological & Cognitive (知識與認知層面)**: The nature of the math itself changes. It becomes more abstract, symbolic, and rigorous. Students must shift from "Calculating" to "Theorizing."
2.  **Sociological & Cultural (社會與文化層面)**: The environment changes. Students lose small-class support and are expected to have high **[[Academic Autonomy]]**. The "culture" of university math (long lectures, independent proof) is alien.
3.  **Didactical (教學層面)**: The teachers and curricula change. High school focuses on guiding; university focuses on presenting. The **[[Didactical Contract]]** is broken.

## Significance
Effective transition programs must have "Three-Way Alignment"—they must support knowledge (tutoring), study skills (culture), and pedagogical continuity.
