# Gueudet 2016 - Productive Discontinuity

## The Concept
Gueudet (2016) challenges the idea that "discontinuity" (the gap) is always a bad thing that should be "removed."

## The Idea
- **Productive Conflict**: A certain level of discontinuity triggers **[[Cognitive Conflict]]**, which is necessary for deep restructuring of knowledge.
- **The Threshold**: If the gap is too small, students just "rely on old habits" (Conceptual stagnation). If the gap is too large, they collapse (Mathematical Crisis).
- **Goal**: To manage the gap such that it becomes a "Productive Opportunity for Learning" where students are forced to develop **[[Advanced Mathematical Thinking]]** to survive.

## Application
Teaching should not just "bridge" the gap by making things easier, but should "scaffold" students *while they are in the gap* so they can climb to the next conceptual level.
