# Tall 1998 - Three Worlds of Mathematics

## The Concept
Tall (1998) proposes that mathematical growth happens across three distinct "cognitive worlds."

## The Three Worlds
1.  **The Conceptual-Embodied World**: Based on perception, action, and visual intuition. (e.g., imagining a "line" as a physical path).
2.  **The Proceptual-Symbolic World**: Based on symbols as both process and concept. (e.g., algebra, arithmetic).
3.  **The Axiomatic-Formal World**: Based on formal definitions, axioms, and logical proof. (e.g., Real Analysis).

## The STT Problem
Secondary math lives mostly in worlds 1 and 2. University math abruptly demands world 3. Many students attempt to solve world 3 problems using world 1/2 logic, leading to the **[[Double Discontinuity]]**.
