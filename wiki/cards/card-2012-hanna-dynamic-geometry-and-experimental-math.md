## Key Insight
The rise of **Computer Algebra Systems (CAS)** and **Dynamic Geometry Software (DGS)** has fundamentally challenged the traditional notion of proof. Key implications for STT:
- DGS allows students to "see" theorems as true before proving them, reinforcing *Empirical Proof Schemes*.
- This creates a "technological discontinuity": students trained with Geogebra or Desmos struggle to transition to the purely symbolic and axiomatic world of university mathematics.
- The field is split: some argue technology supports proof exploration; others warn it undermines students' motivation to construct formal proofs.
