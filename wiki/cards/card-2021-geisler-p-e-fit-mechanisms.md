# Geisler 2021 - P-E Fit Mechanisms

## The Concept
Geisler (2021) applies the **Person-Environment Fit (P-E Fit)** theory to explain the high dropout rates in mathematics.

## The Mechanisms
- **Needs-Supplies Mismatch**: Students enter with a *need* for applied, intuitive math (school culture), but the university *supplies* only abstract, formal proofs.
- **Demands-Abilities Mismatch**: The university *demands* rigorous logic and proof construction, which exceeds the student’s current *abilities* built on procedural algorithms.
- **Consequence**: Perpetual mismatch leads to **Cognitive Stress** and a decline in satisfaction, eventually triggering the "exit" decision.

## Conclusion
A successful transition requires either (a) shifting student expectations (calibration) or (b) temporary modifications in the environment to bridge the mismatch (e.g., [[Calculus Redesign]]).
