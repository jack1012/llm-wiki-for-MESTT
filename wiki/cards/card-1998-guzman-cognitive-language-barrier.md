# Guzman 1998 - Cognitive Language Barrier

## The Concept
A key epistemological difficulty identified by Guzman (1998) is the mismatch between **Natural Language** and **Formal Mathematical Language**.

## The Barrier
- **School**: Math is often explained with analogies and natural language (e.g., "the function gets closer to...").
- **University**: Math is defined through rigorous symbolic strings (e.g., $\epsilon-\delta$ definitions).
- **The Gap**: Students try to read formal symbols as if they were natural sentences, missing the logical structure. They fail to understand that university math *is* its formal language.

## Significance
Bridging the transition requires treating mathematical notation as a "Foreign Language" that students need to learn to *read* and *write* fluently before they can reason within it.
