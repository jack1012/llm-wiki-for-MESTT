## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: STEM student dropout in German higher education follows distinct causal pathways and trajectories, requiring differentiated understanding of why students leave at various transition stages.
- **Research Problem**: To classify and analyze the causes and trajectories of student dropout in German STEM programs, distinguishing between early-transition dropout, performance-based withdrawal, and other departure mechanisms.
- **Key Results**: German STEM dropout follows distinct causal pathways: early-transition dropouts differ from performance-based withdrawals; performance pressure, lack of motivation, and institutional mismatch are primary drivers; dropout rates vary significantly by STEM discipline and are influenced by socioeconomic factors and alternative opportunities.
- **Methodology**: Large-scale quantitative administrative data analysis (DZHW cohort tracking); causal pathway mapping; German higher education system analysis.
