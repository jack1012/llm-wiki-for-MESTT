## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: In Ireland, students in "service mathematics" (e.g., Engineering, Business) enter university with weak foundations, creating the "Mathematics Problem." Most research ignores how students' feelings (affect) and study approaches impact their success.
- **Research Problem**: How do affective variables (enjoyment, self-concept) and learning approaches influence performance in first-year university mathematics?
- **Key Results**: **Mathematical Self-concept** and **Enjoyment** are stronger predictors of success than learning approaches. A critical finding is the lack of **Mathematical Resilience**: students often give up after just five minutes of struggle if they don't see an immediate algorithmic path. Students are "fragile" learners who rely on rote strategies that fail in university.
- **Methodology**: Mixed-methods; Quantitative survey (N=300); Qualitative interviews at an Irish university (University of Limerick context).

## Structured Reading Notes
- **Research Topic**: Affective and cognitive determinants of success in first-year service mathematics.
- **Problem Awareness / Puzzle**: "Why do students with decent school grades fail service mathematics?"
- **Research Questions**: 1) What is the relationship between affect and performance? 2) How does "resilience" manifest in the first year?
- **Theoretical Framework / Perspective**: Affective Domain (McLeod); Deep/Surface Learning (Marton).
- **Core Concepts**: Service Mathematics, Mathematical Resilience, Fragility, Enjoyment.
- **Data / Participants**: 300 first-year students in service mathematics courses (Science, Engineering, Computing).
- **Main Findings**: 
    - Affective variables account for more variance in performance than cognitive ones in the first semester.
    - **Five-Minute Rule**: Many students have an "expiration timer" for effort; if a problem isn't solved in 5-10 minutes, they attribute failure to "lack of ability."
    - **Surface Learning Dominance**: The Leaving Certificate (Irish terminal exam) rewards rote-learning, which leaves students without the tools to handle the conceptual "Rupture" of university math.
- **Discussion / Interpretation**: Service math students often have a **"Utility-Only"** view of math, making them less willing to engage in "struggle" (low resilience).
- **Contribution**: Introduced the concept of "Resilience" to the Irish STT discourse.
- **Limitations**: Specific to the Irish context and "service" (non-major) mathematics.
- **Suggestions / Implications**: Curriculum must foster "Resilience" by rewarding process and persistence, not just correct algorithmic output.

## Career Development Analysis (生涯發展觀點)
> [!IMPORTANT]
> **研究者觀點導入**：Liston (2007) 揭示了 **「服務數學（Service Math）」** 學生在生涯路徑上的 **「工具性焦慮 (Instrumental Anxiety)」**。

*   **生涯層次的體現**：對於非數學主修的學生而言，數學是他們職業生涯中的 **「外部強加工具 (External Tool)」** 而非「內在組成」。這導致了 **「外圍生涯身份 (Peripheral Career Identity)」**：他們不認為自己是「做數學的人」。因此，當數學難度增加時，他們缺乏克服困難的「職業動力」，進而引發「五分鐘放棄」的行為。
*   **文獻忽略之處**：文中提到的「數學韌性 (Mathematical Resilience)」不足，本質上是 **「職業韌性 (Career Resilience)」** 在學科層次的投影。學生若看不見「這道微積分題」如何轉化為其未來身為「生物學家」或「管理者」的專業能力，他們自然會選擇「防禦性放棄（Defensive Dropout）」。
*   **重構方向**：應在服務數學中導入 **「職業連結任務 (Career-connected Tasks)」**，透過賦予數學任務「職涯意義感」來延長學生的「努力耐受期」。
