## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: Students entering university face significant "discontinuities" because they are unprepared for the sudden shift from algorithmic to proof-based mathematics.
- **Research Problem**: To identify the key concepts and pedagogical shifts that students perceive as most challenging during the first year of university mathematics.
- **Key Results**: Highlights the shift from "doing" mathematics (procedures) to "thinking" about mathematics (logic/proof). Identifies the "Gap" as a mismatch in the "didactical contract" between lecturer and student.
- **Methodology**: Qualitative case study; focus groups and interviews with first-year mathematics students.
