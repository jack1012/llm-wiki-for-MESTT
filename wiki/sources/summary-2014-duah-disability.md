## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: Students with disabilities face compound challenges during the transition to university mathematics, combining STT difficulties with accessibility and identity barriers.
- **Research Problem**: To understand how students with disabilities experience and navigate the transition from secondary to tertiary mathematics, and what support mechanisms facilitate their success.
- **Key Results**: Students with disabilities experience compounded barriers combining STT cognitive difficulties with accessibility and emotional support gaps; institutional response is fragmented and often inadequate; effective support requires accessible mathematics content, adjusted expectations, and identity-affirming mentoring.
- **Methodology**: Qualitative narrative inquiry and phenomenological case studies with disabled students transitioning to university mathematics.
