## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: Students often carry "instrumental" views of equations from school that hinder their performance in advanced calculus and linear algebra.
- **Research Problem**: How do first-year university students' perspectives on "equations" differ from the formal expectations of tertiary mathematics?
- **Key Results**: Identifies a spectrum of perspectives: (1) **Action-oriented**: Equation as a command to find a value. (2) **Process-oriented**: Equation as a balanced operation. (3) **Object-oriented**: Equation as a mathematical structure in itself. The transition to university requires a shift to the "Object" view, but most students remain stuck in the "Action" phase, leading to difficulties in generalization and proof.
- **Methodology**: Quantitative survey and qualitative clinical interviews with first-year students.

## Career Development Analysis (生涯發展觀點)
> [!IMPORTANT]
> **研究者觀點導入**：Godfrey (2008) 實際上描述了一種 **「專業工具的誤讀」**。

*   **生涯層次的體現**：數學符號是數學家的「勞動工具」。如果學生將工具誤認為「作業題目（Action）」，他就無法產生 **「專業效能感」**。他在大學感到的「數學成熟度不足」，本質上是他在專業世界中的 **「工具素養」** 尚未達標。
*   **文獻忽略之處**：未探討教學介入如何加速這種認知角色的轉變。
*   **重構方向**：應將 **[[Structural Thinking]]** 視為通往「數學專業身分」的必經之路。
