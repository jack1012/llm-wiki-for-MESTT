## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: Mathematics Support Centers (MSCs) are widely available, but the students who need them most often fail to engage with them due to deep-seated motivational issues.
- **Research Problem**: To investigate the link between students' motivation (based on Self-Determination Theory) and their engagement with Mathematics Learning Support during the transition.
- **Key Results**: Finds that students with "controlled motivation" (driven by external pressure) are less likely to use support services, even if failing. High "autonomous motivation" predicts proactive use of MSCs.
- **Methodology**: Quantitative survey (N=200+) and path analysis at a UK university (Loughborough).
