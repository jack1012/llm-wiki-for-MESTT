## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: Identifying which student-level characteristics (motivation, habits, beliefs) truly matter for university success beyond legacy secondary school grades.
- **Research Problem**: To measure the predictive power of affective variables vs. cognitive pre-knowledge on first-year university mathematics grades.
- **Key Results**: Grade 12 results set an upper limit but not a floor for success. [[Self-efficacy]] measured during the first year is a stronger predictor (21%) than math interest (12%). For struggling students, traditional resources (textbooks/peers) are more effective than internet-based materials.
- **Methodology**: Longitudinal quantitative survey (N=1007) and OPLS regression analysis across three Swedish universities.
