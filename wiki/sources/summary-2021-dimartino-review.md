## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: Fragmented research on secondary-to-tertiary transition in mathematics lacks coherent synthesis, preventing holistic understanding of the phenomenon and effective intervention design.
- **Research Problem**: To systematically review empirical research on the transition from school to university in mathematics education, synthesizing findings across cognitive, affective, and institutional dimensions to establish state-of-the-art knowledge.
- **Key Results**: Systematic review identifies three main research clusters (cognitive, affective, institutional); no single factor predicts success; most research is quantitative/correlational, lacking causal explanation; holistic frameworks integrating multiple dimensions are emerging but remain fragmented; intervention research is underdeveloped relative to description.
- **Methodology**: Systematic literature review (SLR) methodology; coding and thematic synthesis across quantitative, qualitative, and mixed-methods studies; Italian and international literature bases.
