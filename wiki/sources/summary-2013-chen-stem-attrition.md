## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: P4：預測悖論問題
- **Research Problem**: Using the 2004/09 Beginning Postsecondary Students Longitudinal Study (BPS
- **Key Results**: Over 6 years, 48% of bachelor’s and 69% of associate’s STEM entrants left STEM. However, non-STEM fields (e.g., humanities, education) had similar or higher attrition rates. The MNP (Multinomial Probit) analysis revealed that taking lighter STEM credit loads, less challenging math (pre-college instead of calculus), and earning lower STEM grades relative to non-STEM grades were the strongest predictors of switching majors. High-performing students (high GPA) were uniquely prone to switching major
- **Methodology**: Quantitative longitudinal analysis using descriptive statistics and Multinomial Probit (MNP) regression models on a nationally representative sample.
