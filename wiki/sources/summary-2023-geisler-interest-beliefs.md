## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: Cognitive abilities alone do not explain transition success; personal affective resources like interest and beliefs act as critical buffers against stress.
- **Research Problem**: To investigate the interplay between students' mathematical interest and their beliefs (static vs. dynamic) in predicting study persistence.
- **Key Results**: Finds that "Dynamic Beliefs" (math as discovery) foster "University Interest", which in turn reduces dropout intention. Static beliefs are a liability.
- **Methodology**: Quantitative survey; Structural Equation Modeling; N=600+.
