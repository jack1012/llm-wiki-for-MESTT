## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: High school grades have become a "deceptive signal." Students with 80-90% grades often lack the fundamental **Algebraic Flexibility** required to survive university-level Calculus.
- **Research Problem**: How can diagnostic testing and early curriculum intervention mitigate the high withdrawal rates in first-year mathematics?
- **Key Results**: Diagnostic testing at McMaster revealed a massive gap: **40% of students with an "A" in high school** failed the basic algebra diagnostic test. The study finds that students suffer from **"The Confidence Trap"**—they are unaware of their own knowledge gaps. The study advocates for a **Gradual Transition Model**: spending the first 3-4 weeks of the first semester on "re-strengthening" school foundations through a formal university lens. This approach significantly reduced withdrawal rates and improved long-term persistence.
- **Methodology**: Quantitative analysis of diagnostic test data (N=2000+) and longitudinal tracking of course withdrawal/success rates.

    - Early identification of "At-risk" students through low-stakes diagnostic testing is the most effective intervention.
- **Contribution**: Provided empirical evidence that universities must "buffer" the transition through curriculum redesign.

## Career Development Analysis (生涯發展觀點)
> [!IMPORTANT]
> **研究者觀點導入**：Kajander (2005) 揭示了轉銜作為 **「職涯現實感的校準器 (The Calibrator of Career Reality)」** 的必要性。

*   **生涯層次的體現**：文中的「診斷震撼」反映了學生 **「生涯資本 (Career Capital)」** 的虛假膨脹。學生帶著高分進入大學，這是一種 **「自信陷阱 (The Confidence Trap)」**：他們以為自己具備了成為工程師或科學家的門票，但現實卻揭露了其專業基石的脆弱。麥克馬斯特大學的漸進式轉銜模型，實質上是在提供 **「職涯避震器 (Career Shock Absorber)」**。它讓學生在面臨真正的職業挫敗前，有機會進行 **「職涯校準 (Career Recalibration)」**。成功的學生是那些能將「診斷失敗」轉化為「專業化動力」的人。
*   **文獻忽略之處**：未討論這種「現實校準」對低社經地位學生（缺乏心理資源者）的潛在負面衝擊。
*   **重構方向**：應將「代數靈活性」標註為衡量 **「職業預測能力」** 的核心指標。
