## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: Traditional mathematics instruction focuses on procedural fluency ("how") over conceptual "why", leading to critical failure in university transition.
- **Research Problem**: To investigate the effectiveness of using counter-examples as a pedagogical tool to challenge students' logical reasoning and correct common misconceptions.
- **Key Results**: Counter-examples are highly effective in inducing cognitive conflict, forcing students to move beyond rote learning and engage with the underlying logic of mathematics.
- **Methodology**: Action research; analysis of student responses to counter-example tasks in first-year calculus.
