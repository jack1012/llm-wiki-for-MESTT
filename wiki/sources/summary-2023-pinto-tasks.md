## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: Mathematical tasks in the transition are often seen by students as "abstract hurdles" rather than "sense-making opportunities", leading to disengagement.
- **Research Problem**: To examine the "student view" of different types of university tasks (formal vs. applied) and how these views correlate with their study approaches.
- **Key Results**: Students who view tasks as "narratives of discovery" perform better than those who view them as "compliance exercises". The perceived "openness" of a task correlates with positive interest.
- **Methodology**: Qualitative analysis of student reflections on non-routine calculus tasks.
