## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: Mathematics curricula and textbooks often rely on linear, "one-size-fits-all" approaches that marginalize students from diverse backgrounds during the transition.
- **Research Problem**: To examine the role of teacher resources (textbooks, digital tools) in shaping the inclusive potential of first-year mathematics education.
- **Key Results**: Identifies a lack of resources that support "multi-path" learning. High-quality teacher support is needed to bridge the gap between abstract curricula and the diverse lived realities of students.
- **Methodology**: Curricular analysis and teacher interviews across European universities.
