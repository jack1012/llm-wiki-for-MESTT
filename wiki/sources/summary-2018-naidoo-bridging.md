## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: Bridging programmes represent a widespread intervention for STT challenges, yet evidence on their effectiveness and mechanisms of impact remains inconsistent and context-dependent.
- **Research Problem**: To evaluate the impact of a bridging programme on student transition success to university mathematics, examining both immediate outcomes and longer-term performance trajectories in a South African context.
- **Key Results**: Bridging programmes show modest but meaningful improvements in transition success rates; effectiveness varies by programme design (co-requisite vs. standalone) and student population; long-term benefits require sustained institutional support beyond bridging; addressing foundational gaps alone is insufficient without attention to institutional culture shifts.
- **Methodology**: Quasi-experimental intervention design; comparative performance analysis (bridging vs. non-bridging cohorts); South African higher education case study.
