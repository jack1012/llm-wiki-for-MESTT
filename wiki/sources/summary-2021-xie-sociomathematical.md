## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: Sociomathematical norms—community standards for what counts as acceptable mathematical explanation, proof, and reasoning—shift between secondary and tertiary mathematics, but students from collectivist cultures may be unprepared for norm changes.
- **Research Problem**: To investigate how sociomathematical norms develop and shift during the transition to university mathematics in East Asian contexts, examining how cultural orientations (collectivist/individualist) interact with changing mathematical community standards.
- **Key Results**: ## 關鍵發見 (Key Findings)
- **Methodology**: Sociomathematical norms framework (Yackel & Cobb); comparative ethnographic study; Chinese/East Asian higher education context; cultural-comparative analysis.
