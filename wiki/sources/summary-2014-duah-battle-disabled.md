## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: Successful school students often experience the transition as a "battle" where their previous identities as "good at math" are suddenly disabled by new abstract demands.
- **Research Problem**: To explore the metaphorical language students use to describe their struggle with university mathematics and its impact on their self-concept.
- **Key Results**: Finds that the "disabling" effect of university abstraction leads to deep affective crisis. Students feel they have to "fight" to reclaim their mathematical agency.
- **Methodology**: Narrative analysis of student interviews; focus on identity as a trajectory.
