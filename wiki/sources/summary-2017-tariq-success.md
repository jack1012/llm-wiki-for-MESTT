## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: Success in transitioning to university mathematics depends on multiple interacting factors (mathematical competence, motivation, support, subject relevance) that vary by student population and discipline context.
- **Research Problem**: To identify and analyze the key factors that influence success in the transition to university mathematics, particularly in bioscience-dependent programs with mathematically weaker cohorts.
- **Key Results**: Success depends on integrated factors: mathematical competence alone is insufficient without motivation and institutional support; discipline context (bioscience vs. engineering) shapes how mathematics is perceived and learned; targeted support improves outcomes in mathematically weaker cohorts; support timing and relevance matter critically.
- **Methodology**: Mixed-methods case study analysis; qualitative interviews with successful and struggling students; quantitative performance tracking across transition phases.
