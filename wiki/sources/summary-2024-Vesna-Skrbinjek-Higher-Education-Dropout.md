## Core Claims (English Only)

### 1. Problematic
Student dropout remains one of the most significant challenges in global higher education, yet its causes are often studied in isolation. There is a need to synthesize existing research to understand the systemic interplay between individual, institutional, and socioeconomic factors that lead students to abandon their studies.

### 2. Research Problem
What are the multifaceted determinants of higher education dropout according to recent literature, and how can advanced analytical tools (like machine learning) improve our ability to predict and prevent student departure?

### 3. Key Results
The review categorizes dropout factors into four domains: (1) Individual (skills, behavior, demographics); (2) Family (support, background); (3) Institutional (teaching quality, support services); and (4) Societal/Labor Market (workforce demand, financial investment). A key finding is the rising trend of using predictive analytics and machine learning (e.g., Random Forest, Decision Trees) to identify at-risk students earlier than traditional methods allow.

### 4. Methodology
Systematic literature review of recent studies on higher education dropout, with a focus on synthesizing determinants and highlighting the growing role of big data and analytics in retention research.
