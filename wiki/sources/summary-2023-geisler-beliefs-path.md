## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: Students' beliefs about the nature of mathematics often conflict with university expectations, yet the specific longitudinal impact of these beliefs on success is under-researched.
- **Research Problem**: To investigate how first-year students' beliefs (dynamic vs. static) develop and how they predict study success (achievement and satisfaction) across the transition.
- **Key Results**: Dynamic beliefs (math as a process/useful) positively predict success. Static beliefs (math as schematic/product) are problematic. Beliefs undergo significant destabilization and change during the first year.
- **Methodology**: Quantitative longitudinal study, N=611 first-year students, SEM and latent transition analysis.
