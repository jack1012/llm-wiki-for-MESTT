## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: High dropout rates in math programs are often attributed to lack of skill, but the affective path from poor achievement to physical withdrawal is not well understood.
- **Research Problem**: To investigate the underlying affective mechanisms linking students' math interest and self-concept to actual early dropout, specifically testing the mediating roles of study satisfaction and achievement.
- **Key Results**: Dropout is mediated by satisfaction and achievement. Low self-concept reduces satisfaction, which predicts dropout. Interest affects dropout ONLY via satisfaction. This creates a dual-pathway crisis.
- **Methodology**: Quantitative longitudinal study, N=274 first-year students, Path Analysis (Mplus).
