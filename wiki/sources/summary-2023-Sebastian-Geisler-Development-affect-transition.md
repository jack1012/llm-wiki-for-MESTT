## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: Cognitive factors for STT success are well-studied, but the *dynamic* development of affect (how it changes mid-semester) and its relation to dropout remains under-researched.
- **Research Problem**: How do mathematical self-concept and interest change during the first semester, and how do these changes relate to dropout?
- **Key Results**: Dropout students show a sharper decline in **Interest** and **Self-Concept** within the first 10 weeks. The primary triggers are **homework assignments** and **criterial feedback**. Mid-term affective states are more predictive of dropout than entry-level states.
- **Methodology**: Mixed-methods; Longitudinal quantitative (N=556); Qualitative interviews (N=18); Linear Algebra courses in Germany.

## Structured Reading Notes
- **Research Topic**: Longitudinal development of affect and its predictive power for university mathematics dropout.
- **Problem Awareness / Puzzle**: Why do entry-level surveys fail to predict many first-semester dropouts?
- **Research Questions**: 1) How does affect develop longitudinally? 2) What specific situations trigger affective decline?
- **Theoretical Framework / Perspective**: Expectancy-Value Theory (Eccles).
- **Core Concepts**: Mathematical Self-Concept, Intrinsic Value, Utility Value, Criterial Feedback.
- **Data / Participants**: 556 students in German universities; 18 in-depth interviews.
- **Main Findings**: The "Affective Gap" widens significantly by mid-term. The **Homework Cycle** is the most critical learning situation. While all students experience some decline, dropouts experience a **"Crisis of Competence"** triggered by failing to solve homework problems and receiving low marks, which destroys their interest.
- **Discussion / Interpretation**: The transition is a "Battle of Interest." If the decline is not stopped by mid-term, dropout becomes inevitable.
- **Contribution**: Identified the **Mid-term Window** as the critical period for intervention.
- **Limitations**: Focuses on Linear Algebra; results might vary in other subjects like Analysis.
- **Suggestions / Implications**: Intervention should occur at the "Homework and Feedback" level, providing emotional support alongside cognitive help.

## Career Development Analysis (生涯發展觀點)
> [!IMPORTANT]
> **研究者觀點導入**：Geisler (2023) 的數據反映了 **「生涯工具性價值 (Utility Value)」** 在轉銜中的崩潰。

*   **生涯層次的體現**：當學生的「興趣」和「自我概念」在第 10 週崩潰時，他們面臨的是 **「生涯替代路徑」** 的誘惑。如果數學被視為「純粹的興趣」，一旦受挫則興趣全失；但若數學被視為「生涯工具」，學生或許能耐受較長時間的情感低潮。
*   **文獻忽略之處**：Geisler 觀察到「興趣」的下降，但未深挖這份興趣背後的 **「職業導向」**（例如：師培生 vs. 純數生）。師培生對於「大學數學」的興趣下降通常伴隨著對「未來職涯相關性」的懷疑。
*   **重構方向**：應探討 **「生涯韌性」** 如何作為「情感保護因子」。如果學生具備強烈的生涯目標，他們在面對「作業挫折 (Homework Crisis)」時，會更傾向於將其視為「必要的專業訓練」而非「個人能力的否定」。

## Evidence Trace
- **Trace 1**: Students who dropped out reported a mean interest decline from 3.2 to 2.1 (on a 4-point scale) in just 10 weeks (p. 8).
- **Trace 2**: Interviews showed that "criterial feedback" on homework was mentioned as a negative trigger by 16 out of 18 students (p. 11).
