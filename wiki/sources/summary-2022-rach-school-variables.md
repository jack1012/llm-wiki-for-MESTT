## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: School grades (GPA) are often used as entry requirements, but their predictive power for success in abstract university-level mathematics is controversial.
- **Research Problem**: To investigate the extent to which school-related variables (grades, interest, background) predict success in the first year of university.
- **Key Results**: Finds that general school GPA has only a marginal influence compared to specific "Topic-Specific Prior Knowledge". Success is more linked to adaptation than to school past.
- **Methodology**: Quantitative longitudinal study; N=800+.
