## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: Transition success is often analyzed using simple linear models that fail to account for the nested nature of students within specific institutional and departmental contexts.
- **Research Problem**: To use Hierarchical Linear Modeling (HLM) to identify the relative contributions of student-level factors vs. class-level factors on transition achievement.
- **Key Results**: Finds that while prior achievement is the strongest individual predictor, class-level factors (teaching quality, support availability) account for a significant portion of the variance in success.
- **Methodology**: Quantitative longitudinal study using HLM; analysis of nested data from multiple faculties.
