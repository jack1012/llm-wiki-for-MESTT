## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: Mathematical transitions across educational levels (primary-secondary-tertiary) exhibit systemic discontinuities that compound at each juncture, creating cumulative student difficulty.
- **Research Problem**: To systematically survey transitions in mathematics education across multiple levels, mapping the nature of discontinuities (epistemological, pedagogical, institutional) and their effects on student trajectories.
- **Key Results**: Transitions exhibit double discontinuity (epistemological and pedagogical); research has traditionally focused on STT but neglects primary-secondary transitions; systemic understanding requires attention to cumulative discontinuities across multiple transition points; process-oriented perspective enables intervention design across the educational pipeline.
- **Methodology**: ICME-13 topical survey; systematic mapping of transition research; international comparative analysis of secondary-to-tertiary mathematics transitions.
