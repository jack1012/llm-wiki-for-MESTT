## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: P2：制度碰撞問題
- **Research Problem**: How can institutional theories (like ATD) help us understand why students' mathematical behaviors change (or fail to change) across the secondary-tertiary boundary?
- **Key Results**: The study highlights that **Mathematical Practices** are "institutionalized." A student "good at math" in school might fail at university because the **Praxeologies** required are fundamentally different, shifting from procedural techniques to theoretical justifications. It argues for a collective, institutional approach to supporting students.
- **Methodology**: Theoretical analysis using Anthropological Theory of the Didactic (ATD); institutional praxeology examination; comparison of school vs. university mathematical tasks and techniques.
