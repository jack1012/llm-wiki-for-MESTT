# Source: {{title}}

## 🎯 Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: Cognitive variables alone fail to explain why high-attaining school students frequently drop out of university mathematics (up to 80% attrition in some regions).
- **Research Problem**: To investigate the underlying affective mechanisms linking students' math interest and self-concept to actual early dropout, specifically testing the mediating roles of study satisfaction and achievement.
- **Key Results**: 
  - **Mathematical Self-concept** and **Interest in University Mathematics** are significant negative predictors of dropout risk.
  - The relationship between interest and dropout is mediated by **Satisfaction**.
  - The relationship between self-concept and dropout is mediated by both **Satisfaction** and **Achievement**.
  - **Interest in School Mathematics** does not predict program persistence.
- **Methodology**: Quantitative longitudinal study, N=274 first-year students, Path Analysis (Mplus).
