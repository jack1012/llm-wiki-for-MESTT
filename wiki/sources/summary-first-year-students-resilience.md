## 🎯 Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: High dropout rates in the first year are attributed to the "excessive demands" of university math, yet research has lacked a construct to describe how students specifically handle the emotional setbacks of daily assignments.
- **Research Problem**: To adapt the concept of **Mathematical Resilience** to the university level and develop/validate a new instrument (IRME) to measure students' persistence against challenging mathematics exercises.
- **Key Results**:
  - Weekly mathematics assignments (compulsory exercises) are confirmed as the most frustrating and stressful part of the freshmen experience.
  - **Mathematical Resilience** is a distinct and valid construct that significantly predicts student persistence (staying in the program) during the first semester.
  - It is distinguishable from general resilience, conscientiousness, and interest.
- **Methodology**: Quantitative empirical study (N=424), three subsamples (Germany & Sweden), Confirmatory Factor Analysis (CFA) for instrument validation.
