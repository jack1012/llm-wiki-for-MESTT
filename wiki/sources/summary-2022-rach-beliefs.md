## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: Students' beliefs about mathematics (its nature, the required abilities, teaching-learning relationships) are dynamic during transition and significantly influence success, yet are malleable through intervention.
- **Research Problem**: To investigate the role of students' beliefs (epistemological, motivational) in successful transition to university mathematics, and to examine how beliefs change during transition and respond to pedagogical interventions.
- **Key Results**: ## 關鍵發見 (Key Findings)
- **Methodology**: Mixed-methods longitudinal study tracking belief change; belief-change intervention design; German higher education context; ZDM/International Journal framework.
