## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: High school grades are the standard predictor for university success, but they fail to explain the high failure rate of "A-grade" students. We need to understand how beliefs and habits interact with grades.
- **Research Problem**: Which non-cognitive factors (self-efficacy, motivation, study habits) best predict success, and how do their predictive powers change during the first year?
- **Key Results**: Finds that **Self-efficacy** (the belief in one's ability to succeed in specific tasks) is a weak predictor at entrance but becomes the **strongest predictor (21%)** by the end of the first year. This suggests a **"Calibration Effect"** where experience makes student self-assessment more accurate. Jointly, affective factors (motivation + beliefs) have a predictive power nearly equal to secondary school grades. Interestingly, **Study Habits** have low predictive power for the general population but are critical for "at-risk" students, who benefit significantly more from **Textbooks and Peer Interaction** than from internet-based resources.
- **Methodology**: Quantitative longitudinal study across 3 Swedish universities; OPLS regression analysis.

## Structured Reading Notes
- **Theoretical Framework**: Self-Determination Theory (SDT); Expectancy-Value Theory.
- **Core Concepts**: [[Dynamic Self-Efficacy]], [[The Calibration Effect]], [[Autonomous Regulation]], [[Resource-Achievement Link]].
- **Main Findings**: 
    - Entrance beliefs are often "inflated" and uncorrelated with university performance.
    - **Intrinsic Motivation** ("learning for interest") is the most stable predictor of success across all achievement groups.
    - Low-achieving students are often "overwhelmed" by internet resources and need the structure of a textbook.
- **Contribution**: Quantified the "affective-cognitive" balance in STT success.

## Career Development Analysis (生涯發展觀點)
> [!IMPORTANT]
> **研究者觀點導入**：Bengmark (2017) 揭示了 **「職涯自我的精確校準 (Precise Calibration of Career Self)」**。

*   **生涯層次的體現**：轉銜不只是學會數學，更是學會 **「自我評估」**。入學時的「盲目自信」在第一年結束時會被轉化為「基於實力的自我效能感」。這種 **「校準能力」** 是高階職涯中不可或缺的 **「後設認知資產」**。成功的學生能精確知道自己「能做什麼」與「需要補強什麼」。對於「高風險學生」，他們對資源的選擇（捨棄網路碎片資訊、回歸教科書系統）其實是一種 **「職涯防衛機制 (Career Defense Mechanism)」**，用結構化知識來對抗迷惘。
*   **文獻忽略之處**：未討論導師的反饋如何加速這種自我效能感的「校準」過程。
*   **重構方向**：應將「自我效能感的準確度」視為一項 **「職涯成熟度指標」**。
