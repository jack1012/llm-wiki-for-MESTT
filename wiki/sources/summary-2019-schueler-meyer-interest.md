## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: Students' ability to recognize and connect school mathematics to tertiary mathematics depends on discursive affordances and conditions that enable "revisiting" of prior knowledge in new theoretical contexts.
- **Research Problem**: To investigate how students access and reconstruct secondary mathematics concepts within tertiary mathematics contexts, examining the discourse, affordances, and conditions that enable or obstruct such transitions (case: modular arithmetic).
- **Key Results**: ## 關鍵發見 (Key Findings)
- **Methodology**: Commognitive discourse analysis; qualitative study of student mathematical thinking during transition; case-based investigation of modular arithmetic reframing.
