- **Notes**: Investigates the "Affective Rupture" in College Algebra, a major gatekeeper course for higher education persistence.

## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: High failure rates in introductory college mathematics are rooted in deep-seated negative attitudes formed prior to entry, but the institutional role in *reversing* these attitudes is often neglected.
- **Research Problem**: What factors influence college students' attitudes toward mathematics, and how can the classroom environment facilitate a positive shift?
- **Key Results**: Identifies a **Five-Factor Model of Attitude Influence**. The study finds that 80% of these factors are **External**: (1) Teacher Characteristics (the most critical factor), (2) Teaching Style, (3) Classroom Environment, and (4) Assessment Outcomes. The only **Internal Factor** is (5) Individual Perception (the synthesis of prior experiences). A major finding is that the **Personality and Approachability** of the instructor are the primary catalysts for students overcoming math anxiety and re-engaging with the subject.
- **Methodology**: Mixed-methods; Quantitative surveys (N=99) and deep qualitative interviews (N=23) with students in College Algebra courses.

## Structured Reading Notes
- **Theoretical Framework**: Social Cognitive Theory; Attribution Theory.
- **Core Concepts**: [[Attitude toward Mathematics]], [[Teacher Approachability]], [[The Affective Gatekeeper]], [[Five-Factor Model of Attitude]].
- **Main Findings**: 
    - Students who perceived their instructor as "human" and "accessible" showed the most significant positive attitude shifts.
    - Negative attitudes are often linked to a "procedural cage"—being forced to memorize without understanding.
    - Success in the first few weeks of the semester is critical for long-term attitude stability.
- **Contribution**: Shifted the responsibility of student "attitude" from the individual's past to the institution's present pedagogical choices.

## Career Development Analysis (生涯發展觀點)
> [!IMPORTANT]
> **研究者觀點導入**：Goodykoontz (2008) 揭示了 **「職涯路徑的情感保險 (Emotional Insurance for Career Paths)」**。

*   **生涯層次的體現**：對於非數學系（如護理、商學）學生，數學態度是 **「職涯門檻的守門人」**。Goodykoontz 強調的「教師特質」實際上是提供了一種 **「情感保險」**：當學生感到安全時，他們才敢於挑戰那些具備高度失敗風險的學科。在轉銜中克服數學恐懼，本質上是在保留其 **「職涯靈活性 (Career Flexibility)」**。如果學生因為一門代數課而產生永久性的數學厭惡，他們實際上是永久性地關閉了通往 **「高技術回報職涯」** 的大門。
*   **文獻忽略之處**：未探討學生對「數學相關職業薪資」的預期是否能作為一種對抗負面態度的「外部動能」。
*   **重構方向**：應將「數學態度」標註為一項 **「職涯韌性資產 (Career Resilience Asset)」**。
