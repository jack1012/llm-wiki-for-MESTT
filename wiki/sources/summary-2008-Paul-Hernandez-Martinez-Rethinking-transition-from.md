## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: Traditional research frames transition as "trouble" (institutional or cognitive failure), ignoring student agency and the subjective experience of change.
- **Research Problem**: How do students negotiate their identities as they move into post-compulsory mathematics education?
- **Key Results**: Proposes that transition is a **Process of Becoming**. Successful students don't just "overcome gaps"; they actively reconstruct their **Mathematical Identity**. However, certain "bridge" curricula (e.g., Decision Mathematics) can be counterproductive by fostering a procedural identity that conflicts with the rigors of university math.
- **Methodology**: Qualitative interview analysis; sociocultural activity theory framework; part of the Transmaths project (UK).

## Structured Reading Notes
- **Theoretical Framework**: Sociocultural Activity Theory (SCAT); Wenger's Communities of Practice.
- **Core Concepts**: [[Mathematical Identity]], [[Becoming]], [[Deficit vs Identity Model]], [[Identity-in-Practice]].
- **Main Findings**: 
    - Transition is an **"Identity Opportunity"**.
    - Social capital (family/peer support) is the "glue" that holds identity together during cognitive crisis.
    - Students' narratives about math ("I'm a logical person") are more predictive of persistence than their actual grades.
- **Contribution**: Initiated the "Identity Turn" in UK mathematics education research.

## Career Development Analysis (生涯發展觀點)
> [!IMPORTANT]
> **研究者觀點導入**：Hernandez-Martinez (2008) 將轉銜定義為 **「專業身份的重生 (Rebirth of Professional Identity)」**。

*   **生涯層次的體現**：轉銜過程中的「痛苦」並非來自數學題本身，而是來自於 **「舊我（中學優等生）」與「新我（大學探索者）」的衝突**。學生的 **「代理權 (Agency)」** 是他們能否順利完成 **「職涯轉換 (Career Transition)」** 的核心動力。如果學生只被視為「待修補的容器（缺憾模型）」，他會喪失對數學事業的熱情；若被視為「正在生成的研究者（Becoming）」，他則能展現出驚人的轉銜韌性。
*   **文獻忽略之處**：未深入探討如何將這種「認同建構」轉化為具體的校園諮商策略。
*   **重構方向**：應在轉銜教育中加入 **「生涯敘事 (Career Narrative)」** 訓練，幫助學生編織屬於自己的「數學家之路」。
