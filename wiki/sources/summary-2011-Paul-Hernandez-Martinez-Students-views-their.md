## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: Literature often views transition solely as a "problem" or "crisis." This ignores the potential for transition to be a positive "identity project" and an opportunity for personal growth.
- **Research Problem**: How do students perceive the transition from school to college mathematics through the lens of identity and social activity?
- **Key Results**: Students’ narratives often reframe "troubles" as successful "rites of passage." Transition is seen as "growing up," where the increased demands of college (independence, better understanding) provide an opportunity to become a "new person."
- **Methodology**: Qualitative; Biographical interviews; Longitudinal (before and after transition); N=72 total students across two UK projects.

## Structured Reading Notes
- **Research Topic**: Identity construction in the transition to post-compulsory mathematics.
- **Problem Awareness / Puzzle**: Why do some students relish the challenges of transition while others drop out? Is transition just a curriculum gap, or something deeper?
- **Research Questions**: 1) How do students narrate their transition? 2) How is identity mediated by mathematical activity?
- **Theoretical Framework / Perspective**: Cultural Historical Activity Theory (CHAT); Holland’s "Figured Worlds"; Beach’s "Consequential Transitions."
- **Core Concepts**: Leading Identity, Consequential Transition, Figured Worlds, Identity-in-practice.
- **Data / Participants**: 72 UK students (16-18 years old) transitioning to A-level or BTEC mathematics.
- **Main Findings**: Students perceive college math as "harder" but "grown-up." The "newness" of college math provides a space for an identity change. Leading identities (e.g., "becoming an engineer") motivate engagement with difficult content.
- **Discussion / Interpretation**: Transition should be understood as "development" rather than just a "gap." Softening institutional expectations might actually hinder this identity growth.
- **Contribution**: Introduces a positive, agency-oriented discourse to the STT field, balancing the dominant "deficit model."
- **Suggestions / Implications**: Support students to overcome challenges in a developmental way rather than making "college more like school."
