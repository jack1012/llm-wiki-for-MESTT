## 🎯 Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: While entry-level affect is important, the *within-semester development* (trajectory) of interest and self-concept as a predictor for actual dropout remains underexplored, as do the specific triggers for these changes.
- **Research Problem**: Study 1 examines if intra-individual changes in interest and self-concept during the first term predict dropout. Study 2 explores specifically which learning situations lead to the emergence or decline of self-concept.
- **Key Results**:
  - **Affective Trajectory**: A decline in interest and self-concept during the first term is a strong additional predictor of dropout.
  - **Dominance of Criterial Feedback**: Unlike the school context, students adjust their self-concept primarily based on **Criterial Frames of Reference** (weekly homework scores) rather than social comparison.
  - **Situation Criticality**: Working on homework assignments and receiving feedback are the most crucial "Learning Situations" for self-concept development.
- **Methodology**: Mixed-methods. Study 1: Quantitative longitudinal (N=556). Study 2: Qualitative content analysis (N=18).
