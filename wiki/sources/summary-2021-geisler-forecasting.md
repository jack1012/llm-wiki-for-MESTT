## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: Affective variables (interest, self-concept) fluctuate wildly in the first semester, making them difficult to predict or manage for lecturers.
- **Research Problem**: To investigate whether early-semester measurements (pre-test) can reliably forecast students' affective state at the end of the semester.
- **Key Results**: Pre-university self-concept is a weak predictor of mid-semester affect. The "Reality Shock" of the first few weeks is the primary determinant of mid-semester interest decline.
- **Methodology**: Quantitative longitudinal study; N=500+; Autoregressive Cross-Lagged Modeling.
