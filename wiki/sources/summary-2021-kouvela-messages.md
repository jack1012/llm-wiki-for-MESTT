## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: First-year university mathematics students receive multiple, often contradictory messages about mathematical practice, rigor, and expectations from different institutional actors and discourses, creating confusion about what "counts" as doing mathematics.
- **Research Problem**: To analyze the messages and signals that first-year mathematics students receive during their transition to university, examining how institutional framing, pedagogical practices, and Bernstein's code dimensions communicate mathematical expectations.
- **Key Results**: ## 關鍵發見 (Key Findings)
- **Methodology**: Discourse analysis of institutional communication; Bernstein's framework analysis; qualitative interviews with students about received messages; UK higher education context.
