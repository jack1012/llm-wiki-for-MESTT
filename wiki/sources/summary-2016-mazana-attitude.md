## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: Attitude towards mathematics is often measured as a one-dimensional construct (like/dislike), which fails to explain why students who recognize the importance of math still avoid it.
- **Research Problem**: To investigate the multidimensional nature of student attitudes (Affective, Behavioural, Cognitive) and how they evolve during the transition from secondary school.
- **Key Results**: (1) **The ABC Asymmetry**: Students often maintain high **Cognitive** scores (believing math is important for careers) but low **Affective** scores (fear/anxiety). (2) **Behavioral Avoidance**: This mismatch leads to negative **Behavioural** outcomes, such as avoiding tasks or skipping classes. (3) **The Transition Decline**: Attitudes significantly deteriorate as the curriculum moves from basic arithmetic to abstract concepts, mirroring findings in Western contexts but with specific socio-economic pressures in Tanzania.

## Structured Reading Notes (中文摘要)
- **研究主題**: 使用 ABC 模型探討坦尚尼亞學生的數學態度。
- **核心發現**:
    - **ABC 模型**: 態度是由情意 (Affect)、行為 (Behavior) 與認知 (Cognition) 三者交織而成。
    - **態度失調**: 學生可能「理智上」覺得數學很重要（C高），但「情感上」極度排斥（A低），最終導致「行動上」的放棄（B低）。
    - **轉銜衝擊**: 隨著數學抽象度增加，學生的正面態度呈現下降趨勢。
