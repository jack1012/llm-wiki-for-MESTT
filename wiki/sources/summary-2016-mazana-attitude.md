## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: Student attitudes toward mathematics (affective, behavioural, cognitive dimensions) shape engagement and persistence through tertiary transitions, yet attitudes are often overlooked as STT variables.
- **Research Problem**: To investigate the multidimensional structure of students' attitudes toward mathematics and how affective, behavioural, and cognitive components influence transition success and persistence.
- **Key Results**: Student attitudes toward mathematics are multidimensional (affective, behavioral, cognitive) and significantly predict transition success; the ABC model reveals that attitudes are modifiable through targeted interventions; negative attitudes intensify during STT and persist if unaddressed, creating cumulative disadvantage.
- **Methodology**: Quantitative survey using the ABC (Affective-Behavioral-Cognitive) model; correlational analysis of attitude dimensions and transition outcomes.
