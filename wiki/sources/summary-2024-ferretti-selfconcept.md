## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: High dropout rates in STEM subjects, particularly mathematics, are often linked to affective factors. This study focuses on how the transition "shock" destabilizes students' perception of their competence.
- **Research Problem**: How does math self-concept change during the first year? Is there a direct link between this change and dropout thoughts?
- **Key Results**: 79% of students experienced a decline in math self-concept. A strong statistical correlation exists between this decline and dropout intentions.
- **Methodology**: Longitudinal study at the University of Ferrara involving three cohorts (2021-2024). Quantitative data collected via pre- and post-questionnaires.
