## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: Resilience is often viewed as a fixed personality trait, ignoring how socio-cultural deprivation can paradoxically forge autonomy.
- **Research Problem**: How do students from disadvantaged backgrounds transform systemic barriers and school-level "risks" into developmental capital for STT?
- **Key Results**: Resilience is relational and functional. Paradoxically, poor secondary schooling can force students to become independent "math-fathomists," a habitus that is highly valued and successful in the university field.
- **Methodology**: Qualitative narrative analysis (Longitudinal case studies, N=2) using Bourdieusian field theory as a lens.
