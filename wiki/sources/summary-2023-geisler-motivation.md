## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: High dropout in math is linked to negative motivational developments, but the "interaction" between diverse motivational states (Expectancy-Value Theory) is rarely analyzed in a transition context.
- **Research Problem**: To identify different types of motivational profiles (latent classes) among first-year students and track their stability during the transition.
- **Key Results**: Identified distinct profiles (e.g., "High-Expectancy/High-Value", "Low-Expectancy/High-Value"). Students with low expectancy of success are at high risk even if they value mathematics highly.
- **Methodology**: Quantitative longitudinal study; Latent Profile Analysis (LPA).
