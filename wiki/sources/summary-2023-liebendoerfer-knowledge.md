## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: P4：預測悖論問題
- **Research Problem**: To what extent does prior mathematical knowledge (conceptual vs. procedural) predict student success in the first semester of university mathematics, and what learning strategies mediate this relationship?
- **Key Results**: Conceptual prior knowledge is a stronger predictor of university mathematics success than procedural knowledge; learning strategies (particularly self-regulation and metacognitive awareness) mediate the relationship between prior knowledge and success; passive procedural learning from school hinders first-semester performance.
- **Methodology**: Quantitative longitudinal study; measurement of prior conceptual and procedural knowledge; tracking of learning strategies and first-semester performance; regression and mediation analysis of knowledge types as predictors.
