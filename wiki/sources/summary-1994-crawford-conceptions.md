## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: First-year students often hold fragmented conceptions of mathematics as a set of rules, which leads to surface learning and poor performance in abstract university math.
- **Research Problem**: To identify the qualitative range of students' conceptions of mathematics and how they relate to their learning approaches (surface vs. deep).
- **Key Results**: Identified five categories, from "Fragmented" (numbers/rules) to "Cohesive" (logical system). Cohesive conceptions correlate strongly with deep learning and higher achievement.
- **Methodology**: Phenomenographic qualitative study; interviews with 1st-year math students.
