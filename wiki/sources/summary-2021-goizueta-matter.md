## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: STT discourse overlooks socio-political dimensions, including whose mathematics matters, whose transition is supported, and how power structures shape mathematical epistemology and belonging.
- **Research Problem**: To examine the secondary-to-tertiary transition in mathematics from a socio-political perspective, analyzing how argumentation practices, academic authority, and power relations influence student positioning and mathematical agency.
- **Key Results**: Argumentation practices in mathematics reveal power dynamics that marginalize students' voices and mathematical agency; academic authority concentrates in proof-based discourse, excluding applied or alternative mathematical perspectives; systemic inequities in transition outcomes are masked by deficit narratives focused on student deficiency rather than institutional epistemic exclusion.
- **Methodology**: Socio-political discourse analysis; argumentation studies; case study from Chilean higher education context; critical mathematics education framing.
