## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: P3, P9：情感發展的跨文化研究
- **Research Problem**: How do students' affective states (confidence, motivation, anxiety, enjoyment) evolve longitudinally across the secondary-tertiary transition, and how does affect development relate to mathematical identity construction?
- **Key Results**: Affective development during STT shows longitudinal trajectories where confidence and enjoyment decline while anxiety increases; affect development is distinct from achievement trajectories; cultural context (China vs. Western contexts) shapes affective trajectories; mathematical identity formation is intertwined with affect development; targeted affective interventions can reverse decline trajectories.
- **Methodology**: Longitudinal qualitative study; affective trajectory analysis; narrative interviews over multiple semesters; Chinese mathematics education context; identity-affect integration analysis.
