## 🎯 Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: The sudden shift from the school's "front" of mathematics (intuitive, algorithmic) to the university's "back" (axiomatic, formal proofs) creates a mismatch that leads to students feeling they are no longer doing the subject they chose.
- **Research Problem**: To clarify how static and dynamic beliefs about the nature of mathematics develop during the first term and their respective roles in predicting exam achievement and study satisfaction.
- **Key Results**:
  - **Dysfunctional Adjustment**: Dynamic beliefs significantly decrease during the transition, whereas static beliefs remain stable—a direction opposite to what university math requires.
  - **Dynamic Beliefs** are strong negative predictors of dropout intention.
  - **Static Beliefs** (viewing math as unconnected rules) are negative predictors of achievement in Real Analysis.
- **Methodology**: Quantitative longitudinal study, N=104 freshmen, tracking beliefs at start (T1) and mid-term (T2).
