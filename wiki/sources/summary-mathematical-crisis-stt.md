## 🎯 Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: The "insurmountable struggle" faced by high achievers who were excellent in school but experience a sudden "mathematical malaise" at university, suggesting that cognitive competence is not a sufficient predictor of success.
- **Research Problem**: To analyze the transition as a dynamic "rite of passage" through students' own narratives, focusing on their emotional reactions and failure/success attributions.
- **Key Results**:
  - Identification of the **"First-time Syndrome"**: The trauma of experiencing failure, being "one of many," or not understanding a topic for the first time.
  - **Attribution Gap**: Successful students attribute failure to unstable/controllable factors (effort/strategy), while dropout students attribute it to internal/stable factors (lack of innate talent).
  - Transition involves a radical shift in the **View of Mathematics**—from computation to a "secret language" of formal logic.
- **Methodology**: Qualitative narrative study, Bachelor of Mathematics students in Pisa, Italy (N=127 questionnaires, 37 interviews), using the TMA model (Three-dimensional Model of Attitude).
