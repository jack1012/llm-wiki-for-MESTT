## Core Claims (English Only)

### 1. Problematic
The transition from secondary to tertiary mathematics (STT) is often viewed through a purely cognitive lens, neglecting the complex social, emotional, and cultural shifts students undergo. Standard assessments fail to capture the internal "identity shock" and adjustment processes that occur during this pivotal phase.

### 2. Research Problem
How do students experience the transition to university mathematics when viewed as a "rite of passage," and how can the use of personal journals provide educators with deeper insights into students' cognitive, social, and cultural challenges?

### 3. Key Results
Using the **Rite of Passage** framework, the study reveals that STT involves three phases: separation, liminality, and incorporation. Personal journals proved to be a powerful "diagnostic tool" for educators, uncovering that students' struggles are deeply intertwined with their changing self-concept and the shift in institutional norms. The act of journal writing itself facilitated metacognition and helped students navigate the "liminal" space between being a school student and a university student.

### 4. Methodology
Qualitative analysis of personal journals kept by first-year Architecture students at the **Politecnico di Milano**. The researchers coded the journals to identify themes related to emotional regulation, social integration, and cognitive adjustment.
