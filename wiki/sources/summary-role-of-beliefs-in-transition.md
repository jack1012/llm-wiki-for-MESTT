## 🎯 Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: Traditional success indicators (exam grades) are "messy constructs" that neglect the role of students' underlying beliefs about the nature of mathematics, which can lead to high attrition even among high achievers.
- **Research Problem**: To analyze how static vs. dynamic beliefs about mathematics at the start and middle of the first semester predict objective (achievement, dropout) and subjective (satisfaction) success.
- **Key Results**:
  - **Dynamic Beliefs** (viewing math as creative/process-oriented) are strong positive predictors of satisfaction and achievement.
  - **Static Beliefs** (toolbox view: math as a set of rules) act as a negative predictor and contribute to the "beliefs-overhang" effect.
  - Beliefs measured in the middle of the term (T2) are more robust predictors than entry-level beliefs (T1).
- **Methodology**: Quantitative longitudinal study, N=602 first-year students, hierarchical regression analysis.
