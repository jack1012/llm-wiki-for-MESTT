## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: Students transitioning to university mathematics encounter domain-specific challenges rooted in epistemological shifts and conflicting conceptions of mathematical practice and rigor.
- **Research Problem**: To identify and characterize the key challenges and success factors that university students experience during the transition from secondary mathematics, examining both cognitive and epistemological obstacles.
- **Key Results**: Challenges include epistemological conflicts (proof vs. calculation), cognitive gaps in abstraction, and affective factors (confidence collapse); success factors include metacognitive awareness, social support networks, and alignment with career goals; context-dependent challenges suggest need for tailored institutional responses.
- **Methodology**: Qualitative case study with interviews and focus groups; phenomenological analysis of student transition experiences; West Indies institutional context.
