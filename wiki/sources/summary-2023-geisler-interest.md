## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: General "mathematical interest" is often measured, but the distinction between "school math interest" and "university math interest" is critical for explaining first-year dropout.
- **Research Problem**: To investigate the multi-dimensional structure of mathematical interest and how different interest facets affect study success in the first semester.
- **Key Results**: Identifies that interest in *formal* mathematics is the only significant predictor of persistence. "School-like" interest (calculations) remains stable but does not prevent dropout from abstract courses.
- **Methodology**: Quantitative survey; N=556; Confirmatory Factor Analysis (CFA).
