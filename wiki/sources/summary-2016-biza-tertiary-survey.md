## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: Tertiary mathematics education research lacks comprehensive synthesis across cognitive, sociocultural, and institutional dimensions of student learning and transition.
- **Research Problem**: To synthesize and map the state-of-the-art in research on tertiary mathematics teaching and learning, identifying key theoretical frameworks, methodologies, and remaining gaps.
- **Key Results**: The state-of-the-art in tertiary mathematics education encompasses three main paradigms: transmission-based, learner-centered, and sociocultural approaches; research reveals multiple discontinuities between school and university; communities of practice framework offers integrative lens for understanding learning environments and professional development.
- **Methodology**: Systematic literature review and meta-analytical synthesis of tertiary mathematics education research; ICME-13 topical survey methodology.
