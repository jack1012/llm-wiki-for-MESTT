## Core Claims (English Only)

### 1. Problematic
Despite its foundational importance, mathematics remains a subject where many students face persistent failure. Research identifies various isolated causes, but there is a need for a systematic synthesis of the diverse cognitive, emotional, and institutional factors that create barriers to effective learning, especially during the transition to higher-level mathematics.

### 2. Research Problem
What are the primary themes and categories of difficulties in learning mathematics identified in recent literature, and how do they interact to affect student achievement?

### 3. Key Results
The systematic review identifies five core dimensions of difficulty: (1) **Cognitive Barriers**—challenges with abstract conceptual understanding and procedural fluency; (2) **Mathematics Anxiety**—the emotional strain and negative self-perception that hinders performance; (3) **Pedagogical Factors**—ineffective teaching methods that prioritize rote memorization; (4) **Socio-cultural Factors**—the impact of home environment and societal expectations; and (5) **Resource Limitations**—lack of access to quality materials and support. The review emphasizes that mathematics anxiety often exacerbates cognitive barriers, creating a negative feedback loop.

### 4. Methodology
Systematic literature review following PRISMA guidelines. The study analyzed recent academic publications to categorize and synthesize reported student difficulties in mathematics across various educational levels.
