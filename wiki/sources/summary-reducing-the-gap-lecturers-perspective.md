## 🎯 Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: The persistent 50% failure rate in first-year university mathematics caused by a fundamental mismatch between different stakeholders' views (teachers, students, lecturers) on the nature of mathematics.
- **Research Problem**: To systematize the perspectives of 63 international university lecturers on the specific causes of the gap and the effectiveness of current remedies.
- **Key Results**: 
  - 72% of lecturers identify the "Higher Level of Thinking" required at university as the primary gap.
  - School culture focuses on rote learning of disjointed facts and algorithms, while university requires precision, proof, and conceptual understanding.
  - Communication gap: University and school sectors have almost no overlap or mutual understanding of curricula.
- **Methodology**: Survey (Questionnaire with open-ended questions), 63 respondents from 24 countries.
