## Core Claims (English Only)

- **Problematic**: For mathematics teacher education students, the STT is often marked by an "irrelevance shock." The abstract nature of university math seems disconnected from their future career as school teachers, leading to declining motivation and high dropout rates.

- **Research Problem**: Can the inclusion of **Profession-Related Tasks (PRTs)**—tasks that explicitly connect university-level concepts to school-level content—enhance students' motivational and behavioral states in real-time?

- **Key Results**: PRTs significantly increase students' **Utility Value** and **Intrinsic Value** compared to traditional abstract tasks. Crucially, they also serve to **Lower Perceived Costs** (the emotional and effort-drain costs). While PRTs don't automatically increase effort for everyone, they have the strongest positive effect on students with a high initial interest in school mathematics, helping them maintain engagement through the transition.

- **Methodology**: Experience Sampling Method (ESM) / Task-based questionnaire within uni mathematics courses for pre-service teachers. Comparison of motivational states during PRTs vs. standard mathematical tasks.
