## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: Students transitioning to university mathematics in the UK and Ireland encounter significant preparedness gaps that undermine success despite adequate prior achievement.
- **Research Problem**: To investigate whether students entering UK and Ireland universities are adequately prepared for tertiary-level mathematics, and to identify specific competency gaps affecting the transition.
- **Key Results**: UK and Ireland students show significant preparedness gaps despite adequate prior achievement; core competency deficits in proof, abstract reasoning, and mathematical communication are prevalent; systemic disconnection between school exam-focused pedagogy and university mathematics demands is a primary barrier.
- **Methodology**: Quantitative and comparative analysis of student performance, preparedness metrics, and transition outcomes across UK and Ireland institutions.
