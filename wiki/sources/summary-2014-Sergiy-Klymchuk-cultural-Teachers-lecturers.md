## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: The transition from secondary to tertiary mathematics is often viewed as a cognitive gap in student knowledge, but it is fundamentally a **Cultural Gap**—a misalignment of institutional norms, pedagogical values, and assessment cultures.
- **Research Problem**: To what extent do secondary teachers and university lecturers understand each other's constraints and expectations? How does this "mutual blindness" affect student transition?
- **Key Results**: Identifies a significant **Lack of Mutual Awareness**. Teachers prioritize "procedural success" and "mark-earning" to satisfy school metrics (NCEA), while lecturers demand "conceptual depth" and "autonomous learning." This results in **Cultural Friction**: teachers perceive lecturers as "indifferent" to student struggles, while lecturers perceive teachers as "over-protective," preventing students from developing the independence required for university success. The "Didactic Contract" is shattered during the transition because students carry school-based expectations into a university culture that does not recognize them.
- **Methodology**: Survey and interview analysis of 117 secondary teachers and 26 university lecturers in New Zealand.

## Structured Reading Notes
- **Theoretical Framework**: Brousseau's Didactic Contract; Activity Theory.

- **Main Findings**: 
    - Secondary teachers are forced to "teach to the test," which conflicts with university expectations of structural understanding.
    - Lecturers' lack of awareness about school curriculum leads to a "sink or swim" environment for freshmen.
    - The transition requires a **"Culture Shift"** in student identity, from "passive recipient" to "active scholar."
- **Contribution**: Defined the transition not as a knowledge deficit, but as a **Institutional Culture Clash**.

## Career Development Analysis (生涯發展觀點)
> [!IMPORTANT]
> **研究者觀點導入**：Klymchuk (2014) 揭示了 **「自主性的職業文化衝擊 (The Culture Shock of Autonomy)」**。

*   **生涯層次的體現**：轉銜過程本質上是 **「職業成熟度 (Career Maturity)」** 的突擊檢查。中學環境的「過度保護」實際上是在延緩學生的職業社會化進程。當學生進入大學，面對講師對「自主學習」的絕對要求時，他們經歷的不是學術困難，而是 **「專業身份的崩解」**。Klymchuk 提到的「文化鴻溝」實際上是 **「初階勞動力」與「高階專業人才」** 兩套評價體系的碰撞。成功的學生是那些能迅速辨識並適應新 **「職業文化」** 的人。
*   **文獻忽略之處**：未討論導師（Mentor）如何作為這道文化鴻溝的「擺渡人」。
*   **重構方向**：應將「文化適應力」視為衡量學生 **「職涯遷移能力」** 的核心指標。
