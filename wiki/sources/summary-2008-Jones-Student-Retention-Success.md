## Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: High rates of non-progression (withdrawal) in the first year represent a significant loss for both students and institutions. Traditional support is often reactive and stigmatizing.
- **Research Problem**: What does the existing research evidence suggest are the most effective strategies for improving student retention and success?
- **Key Results**: Identifies that retention is driven by **Academic and Social Integration**. Key levers for success include: (1) **Integrated Support**: Skills development embedded in the curriculum. (2) **Proactive Personal Tutoring**: Moving from "come if you have a problem" to "we check in with you." (3) **Peer-Assisted Study**: The high impact of peer mentoring on belonging. (4) **Mainstream Support**: Interventions work best when they are available to all, not just those labeled "at-risk."
- **Methodology**: Systematic synthesis of UK and international retention research (2002–2008).

    - Social belonging is as important as cognitive readiness for first-year success.
- **Contribution**: Provided an evidence-based "strategic framework" for UK universities to move toward holistic retention policies.

## Career Development Analysis (生涯發展觀點)
> [!IMPORTANT]
> **研究者觀點導入**：Jones (2008) 提出了 **「制度性職涯支持 (Institutional Career Support)」** 的雛形。

*   **生涯層次的體現**：文中提到的「選錯課程 (Wrong Course)」本質上是 **「生涯探索 (Career Exploration)」** 的失敗。轉銜的成功不僅在於留住學生，更在於幫助學生確認其 **「職涯路徑與學業選擇的適配性 (Person-Environment Fit)」**。如果大學的支持系統能轉向「主流化」與「主動化」，它實際上是在為所有學生提供一個 **「職涯安全網」**，降低了因初次選課失誤而導致的職涯中斷風險。
*   **文獻忽略之處**：未討論「職涯焦慮」如何具體轉化為學業撤離的過程。
*   **重構方向**：應將「留任支持」與「生涯諮商」深度整合，將轉銜視為 **「職涯適應 (Career Adaptability)」** 的第一場實戰。
