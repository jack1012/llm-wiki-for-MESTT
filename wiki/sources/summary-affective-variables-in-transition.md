## 🎯 Core Claims (English Only)
- **Problematic**: German math programs suffer from an 80% attrition rate, with 73% failing calculus exams. Identifying which affective states differentiate "at-risk" students from "successful" ones is crucial for institutional support.
- **Research Problem**: To evaluate the relative predictive power of domain-specific (math interest, self-concept) vs. general (self-efficacy, basic needs) affective variables on two success indicators: exam attendance (dropout) and exam outcome (grades).
- **Key Results**:
  - **Exam Attendance** (Persistence) is best predicted by **Interest in Mathematics**.
  - **Exam Outcome** (Achievement) is best predicted by **Continuous Achievement during the semester** (mini-tests), with General Self-efficacy acting as a secondary predictor.
  - Mathematics-related affective variables generally provide more insight than "general" psychological variables in this specific transition context.
- **Methodology**: Quantitative survey (N=193), longitudinal tracking of Calculus I students at Ruhr-University Bochum, multiple regression analysis.
